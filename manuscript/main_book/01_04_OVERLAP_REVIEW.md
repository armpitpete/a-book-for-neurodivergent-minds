# Chapter 1 and Chapter 4 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed:

- Chapter 1 — Start Here When Everything Feels Like Too Much
- Chapter 4 — Overload

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable and useful.

The chapters should not be merged.

They share some language because both need to work for tired, overloaded, neurodivergent readers and young people.

The difference is clear enough if future edits protect each chapter's job.

## Chapter 1 job

Chapter 1 is the doorway chapter.

It is for any reader who feels like everything is too much and needs immediate first help.

Its job is broad:

- reduce one pressure
- make the next step smaller
- stop shame from becoming another load
- help the reader start anywhere, even without exact words

Chapter 1 should stay general.

It should not become a detailed overload chapter.

## Chapter 4 job

Chapter 4 is the overload chapter.

It is for the specific pattern where load builds up and the final trigger is not the whole cause.

Its job is specific:

- explain cumulative overload
- teach the shelf/straw idea
- name different kinds of load
- link to Tool 8 — Noise Budget

Chapter 4 should keep the line:

> Do not only ask what set me off. Ask what had already filled me up.

## Shared material that is allowed

Both chapters may mention:

- pressure
- reducing input
- reducing demand
- stabilising the body
- shame not being useful
- one small next step

This is allowed because both chapters must stand alone.

## Risk

The main risk is repetition.

Chapter 1 should not list too many load categories.

Chapter 4 should not spend too long as a general bad-day opening chapter.

## Editing rule

When editing Chapter 1, keep it as first aid.

When editing Chapter 4, keep it as overload education and load-mapping.

## Status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Keep both chapters as separate cleaned drafts.
