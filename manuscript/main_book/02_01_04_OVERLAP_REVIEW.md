# Chapter 2, Chapter 1, and Chapter 4 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 2 against:

- Chapter 1 — Start Here When Everything Feels Like Too Much
- Chapter 4 — Overload

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 2 has its own job and should stay between Chapter 1 and the later state chapters.

## Chapter 1 job

Chapter 1 is first aid.

Its job is:

- reduce one pressure
- make the next step smaller
- stop shame from becoming another load
- help the reader when they have distress but not words

Chapter 1 should stay broad and immediate.

## Chapter 2 job

Chapter 2 is recognition.

Its job is:

- explain hidden effort
- explain why visible performance can hide cost
- reduce shame from years of being misread
- give the reader the question: what does it cost me?

Chapter 2 should not become another first-aid chapter.

## Chapter 4 job

Chapter 4 is overload education.

Its job is:

- explain cumulative load
- teach the shelf/straw idea
- name different kinds of load
- link to Tool 8 — Noise Budget

Chapter 4 should not become a general hidden-effort chapter.

## Shared language that is allowed

These ideas can appear in more than one chapter:

- looking fine is not the same as being fine
- hidden effort is real
- shame is not useful data
- cost matters
- reduce pressure before pushing harder

This is allowed because each chapter must work on its own.

## Main risk

The main risk is that Chapter 2 could become too close to Chapter 4 if it lists too many load categories.

Keep Chapter 2 focused on hidden effort and cost.

Keep Chapter 4 focused on overload and load-mapping.

## Editing rule

Chapter 1 = what helps right now.

Chapter 2 = why it may have felt harder than it looked.

Chapter 4 = what overload is and how load builds.

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 2 is structurally sound as the recognition bridge.
