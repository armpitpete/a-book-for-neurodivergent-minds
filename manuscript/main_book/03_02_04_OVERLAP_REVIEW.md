# Chapter 3, Chapter 2, and Chapter 4 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 3 against:

- Chapter 2 — Why Life May Have Felt Harder Than It Looked
- Chapter 4 — Overload

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 3 has its own job as the bridge between hidden effort and overload.

## Chapter 2 job

Chapter 2 is recognition.

Its job is:

- explain hidden effort
- explain why visible performance can hide cost
- reduce shame from years of being misread
- give the reader the question: what does it cost me?

Chapter 2 should stay focused on cost.

## Chapter 3 job

Chapter 3 is reframing.

Its job is:

- move the reader away from “I am broken”
- offer load, mismatch, and missing support as better first explanations
- prepare the reader for the more detailed overload chapter
- give the reader the question: what load am I carrying?

Chapter 3 should stay broad and reassuring without becoming vague.

## Chapter 4 job

Chapter 4 is overload education.

Its job is:

- explain cumulative load
- teach the shelf/straw idea
- name different kinds of load
- link to Tool 8 — Noise Budget

Chapter 4 should stay more specific and practical than Chapter 3.

## Shared language that is allowed

These ideas can appear in more than one chapter:

- looking fine is not the same as being fine
- hidden effort is real
- shame is not useful data
- cost matters
- load matters
- support matters

This is allowed because each chapter must work on its own.

## Main risk

The main risk is that Chapter 3 uses the word “load” often enough that it may feel close to Chapter 4.

Keep the distinction:

- Chapter 3 = broad reframe from brokenness to load and support.
- Chapter 4 = specific overload explanation and load-mapping.

## Editing rule

Chapter 2 = what did it cost?

Chapter 3 = what am I carrying?

Chapter 4 = how is the load building?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 3 is structurally sound as the bridge between Chapter 2 and Chapter 4.
