# Chapter 3 Brief

## Title

You Are Not Broken, But You May Be Overloaded

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should give the reader a better explanation than self-blame.

It should help them understand that the issue may be load, mismatch, and repeated strain rather than personal failure.

## Core message

You are not broken.

You may be carrying too much, too often, with too little support.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I am too sensitive.
- I am lazy.
- I am dramatic.
- I am not trying hard enough.
- I should cope like everyone else.
- I must be broken if ordinary life feels this hard.

This chapter should offer a clearer, kinder, more useful explanation.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of load and mismatch
- examples from school, work, home, public places, and relationships
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for explaining load without overexplaining
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

This chapter is not saying nothing is difficult.

It is saying the difficulty may have a better explanation than personal failure.

## Suggested scripts

“I am not broken. I am carrying too much right now.”

“This is not about not trying. This is about too much load.”

“I need the load reduced, not a lecture.”

“I can do more when the conditions are clearer and safer.”

## Link forward

Chapter 4 will explain overload in more detail.

Chapter 3 only needs to prepare the reader for that idea.
