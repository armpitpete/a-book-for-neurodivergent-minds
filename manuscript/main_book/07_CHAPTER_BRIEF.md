# Chapter 7 Brief

## Title

Burnout

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain burnout as longer-term depletion after too much strain for too long.

It should not treat burnout as laziness, weakness, or ordinary tiredness.

## Core message

Burnout is what can happen when the system has been pushed past recovery for too long.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I should be able to bounce back.
- I used to do more than this.
- I am lazy now.
- I am broken now.
- Rest is not fixing it fast enough.
- I cannot tell whether this is tiredness, depression, overload, or failure.

This chapter should give a clear, cautious, shame-free explanation.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- difference between ordinary tiredness and burnout
- explanation of recovery debt
- examples from school, work, home, relationships, and masking
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for reduced demand and recovery time
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

Tiredness often improves with ordinary rest.

Burnout may need reduced demand, protected recovery, and longer-term changes.

## Suggested scripts

“I am not just tired. I think I am burnt out.”

“I need demand reduced, not just encouragement to push through.”

“I cannot recover if the same load keeps coming back.”

“I need a smaller version of the plan.”
