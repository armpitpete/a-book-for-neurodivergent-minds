# Chapter 7 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 7 — Burnout

## Place in book

Chapter 7 follows the short state sequence:

- Chapter 4 — Overload
- Chapter 5 — Shutdown
- Chapter 6 — Meltdown

Chapter 7 should explain what happens when strain continues over time.

## Chapter job

Chapter 7 should explain burnout as longer-term depletion.

It should help the reader understand that burnout is not just being tired after one hard day.

It is what can happen when too much load, too much masking, too little recovery, and too much pressure continue for too long.

## Core point

Burnout is what can happen when the system has been pushed past recovery for too long.

## What Chapter 7 must do

- explain burnout in plain language
- separate burnout from ordinary tiredness
- connect burnout to overload, masking, demand, and recovery debt
- reduce shame
- avoid promising fast recovery
- give small practical actions for now
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapters 4 to 6

Chapter 4 is about load becoming too much.

Chapter 5 is about the system pulling functions down.

Chapter 6 is about visible distress and loss of containment.

Chapter 7 is about long-term depletion after too much strain for too long.

## Suggested structure

1. What this chapter is for
2. If you can only read one thing
3. Start here if
4. Short version
5. What burnout means
6. How burnout can build
7. Common signs
8. What makes it worse
9. What helps
10. 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes
11. Useful scripts
12. What not to force
13. Quick summary

## Avoid

- treating burnout as laziness
- making recovery sound quick or simple
- giving too many actions
- turning the chapter into a productivity plan
- suggesting the reader can think their way out of it
- repeating all of Chapters 4 to 6
