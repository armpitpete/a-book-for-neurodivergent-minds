# Chapter 8 Brief

## Title

The Cost of Masking

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain masking as hidden effort.

It should show that masking can be protective, costly, exhausting, and confusing.

It should not tell the reader to stop masking everywhere.

## Core message

Masking may help you get through a situation, but it can still have a cost.

That cost matters.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I only count as acceptable when I hide parts of myself.
- I do not know who I am when I stop performing.
- I seem fine in public and collapse later.
- I copy other people to know what to do.
- I force eye contact, tone, speech, smiles, or stillness.
- I am exhausted from acting normal.
- I feel guilty for masking and scared not to mask.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain definition of masking
- reasons people mask
- costs of masking
- safety note about not unmasking everywhere
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for reducing masking or asking for safer conditions
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

Masking is not simply lying.

It is often a survival or safety strategy.

But a strategy can still have a cost.

## Suggested scripts

“I am using a lot of effort to seem fine.”

“I need to drop some of the performance.”

“I can communicate better if I do not have to act normal at the same time.”

“I need a safer place to be more honest.”
