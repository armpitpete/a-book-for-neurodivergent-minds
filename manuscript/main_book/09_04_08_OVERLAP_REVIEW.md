# Chapter 9, Chapter 4, and Chapter 8 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 9 against:

- Chapter 4 — Overload
- Chapter 8 — The Cost of Masking

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 9 has its own job as the sensory-load chapter.

## Chapter 4 job

Chapter 4 explains overload broadly.

Its job is:

- explain how load builds
- teach the shelf/straw idea
- show why the final trigger is not always the whole cause

Chapter 4 should stay broad.

## Chapter 9 job

Chapter 9 focuses on one major kind of load: sensory input.

Its job is:

- explain input cost
- name common input types
- show how input can build up
- give small ways to reduce input

Chapter 9 should not become a full repeat of the overload chapter.

## Chapter 8 job

Chapter 8 explains masking.

Its job is:

- explain hiding or performing to seem acceptable or safe
- show the cost of that performance
- keep safety central

Chapter 9 can mention hiding sensory distress, but should not become the masking chapter.

## Main distinction

Chapter 4 = how load builds.

Chapter 8 = what am I forcing myself to hide or perform?

Chapter 9 = what input is my system trying to process?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 9 is structurally sound as the sensory-load chapter.
