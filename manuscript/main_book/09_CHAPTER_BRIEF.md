# Chapter 9 Brief

## Title

Sensory Load

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain sensory load as real load.

It should help the reader recognise sensory input as something that can use capacity, increase stress, and contribute to overload or shutdown.

## Core message

Sensory load is real load.

Reducing input is practical, not weak.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I am too sensitive.
- I should be able to ignore it.
- Other people do not seem bothered.
- I am childish for needing quieter, softer, darker, looser, or less crowded conditions.
- I am making a fuss.
- I should push through sensory distress.

This chapter should give a clearer explanation.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain definition of sensory load
- examples of sound, light, smell, texture, touch, temperature, movement, visual clutter, and internal body sensations
- how sensory load can build quietly
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for reduced input
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

A sensory need is not always a preference.

Sometimes it is a capacity issue.

## Suggested scripts

“This is too much input for me right now.”

“I need less noise / light / touch / smell / movement.”

“I can cope better if we reduce one sensory load.”

“This is not fussiness. It is affecting my capacity.”
