# Chapter 9 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 9 — Sensory Load

## Place in book

Chapter 9 follows:

- Chapter 8 — The Cost of Masking

It should explain one of the major kinds of load that can drive overload, shutdown, meltdown, burnout, masking, avoidance, and recovery debt.

## Chapter job

Chapter 9 should explain sensory load in plain language.

It should help the reader understand that sound, light, smell, texture, touch, movement, temperature, visual clutter, and body sensations can all use capacity.

It should reduce shame around being called “too sensitive.”

## Core point

Sensory load is real load.

If your system is processing too much input, reducing that input is practical, not weak.

## What Chapter 9 must do

- explain sensory load clearly
- avoid calling the reader too sensitive
- name different kinds of sensory input
- show how sensory load can build quietly
- connect sensory load to overload, masking, burnout, and recovery
- give small practical reduction steps
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 4

Chapter 4 explains overload broadly.

Chapter 9 focuses on one major source of load: sensory input.

Chapter 9 can link to overload, but should not retell the whole overload chapter.

## Difference from Chapter 8

Chapter 8 explains masking as a social/safety performance cost.

Chapter 9 explains sensory processing cost.

The two may overlap when someone hides sensory distress.

## Suggested structure

1. What this chapter is for
2. If you can only read one thing
3. Start here if
4. Short version
5. What sensory load means
6. Different kinds of sensory input
7. How sensory load builds
8. What makes it worse
9. What helps
10. 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes
11. Useful scripts
12. What not to force
13. Quick summary

## Avoid

- saying the reader is too sensitive
- making sensory needs sound childish
- treating sensory distress as preference only
- telling the reader to tolerate everything
- giving too many tools at once
- repeating all of Chapter 4
