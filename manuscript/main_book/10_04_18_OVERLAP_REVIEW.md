# Chapter 10, Chapter 4, and Chapter 18 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 10 against:

- Chapter 4 — Overload
- Chapter 18 — Planning Without Collapse

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 10 has its own job as the demand-friction chapter.

## Chapter 4 job

Chapter 4 explains overload broadly.

Its job is:

- explain how load builds
- teach the shelf/straw idea
- show why the final trigger is not always the whole cause

Chapter 4 should stay broad.

## Chapter 10 job

Chapter 10 focuses on demand pressure.

Its job is:

- explain why a task can become harder when it becomes a demand
- show how requests, choices, vague steps, rushing, watching, interruption, and pressure can create friction
- separate friction from refusal
- make the first step smaller and clearer

Chapter 10 should not become a full task-management chapter.

## Chapter 18 job

Chapter 18 will explain planning without collapse.

Its likely job is:

- make plans smaller and safer
- reduce planning overload
- avoid all-or-nothing planning
- help the reader choose practical next steps over full life rebuilds

Chapter 10 can prepare for Chapter 18, but should not take over its planning role.

## Main distinction

Chapter 4 = how load builds.

Chapter 10 = what makes this demand hard to enter?

Chapter 18 = how can I plan without collapsing?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 10 is structurally sound as the demand-friction chapter.
