# Chapter 11, Chapter 10, and Chapter 18 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 11 against:

- Chapter 10 — Demand Friction
- Chapter 18 — Planning Without Collapse

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 11 has its own job as the transition/change chapter.

## Chapter 10 job

Chapter 10 explains demand pressure.

Its job is:

- explain why a task can become harder when it becomes a demand
- show how requests, choices, vague steps, rushing, watching, interruption, and pressure can create friction
- separate friction from refusal
- make the first step smaller and clearer

Chapter 10 should stay focused on demand pressure.

## Chapter 11 job

Chapter 11 explains transition cost.

Its job is:

- explain why change can break the route the brain was using
- show task, place, plan, timing, attention, social mode, and body-state changes
- explain why warning and reset gaps help
- make the new first step easier to find

Chapter 11 should not become a full planning chapter.

## Chapter 18 job

Chapter 18 will explain planning without collapse.

Its likely job is:

- make plans smaller and safer
- reduce planning overload
- avoid all-or-nothing planning
- help the reader choose practical next steps over full life rebuilds

Chapter 11 can prepare for Chapter 18, but should not take over its planning role.

## Main distinction

Chapter 10 = what makes this demand hard to enter?

Chapter 11 = what route just got broken?

Chapter 18 = how can I plan without collapsing?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 11 is structurally sound as the transition/change chapter.
