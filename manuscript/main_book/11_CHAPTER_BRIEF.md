# Chapter 11 Brief

## Title

Transitions, Change, and Being Thrown Off

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain transition cost.

It should help the reader understand why changing task, place, plan, timing, attention, expectation, or social mode can feel hard even when the change looks small.

## Core message

A change can look small from the outside and still break the route your brain was using.

Needing warning, time, or a reset is practical, not childish.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I am too rigid.
- I should cope with change better.
- I overreact when plans change.
- I get thrown off by interruptions.
- I hate switching tasks.
- I need warning before changes.
- I cannot restart quickly once the route is broken.
- People think I am difficult when I am actually disoriented.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain definition of transition cost
- examples of task, place, timing, plan, attention, social, and body-state changes
- why warning and reset time help
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for warning, time, and one change at a time
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

Transition distress is not automatically bad attitude.

It can mean the brain has lost the route it was using and needs a reset.

## Suggested scripts

“I need warning before the change.”

“I need a minute to switch.”

“That change broke the route I was using.”

“Please give me one change at a time.”
