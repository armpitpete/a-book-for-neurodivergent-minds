# Chapter 12 Brief

## Title

Uneven Skills, Uneven Days

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain uneven capacity.

It should help the reader understand why they may be able to do something one day, in one place, or under one condition, but not another day, place, or condition.

## Core message

Being able to do something sometimes does not mean you can do it always.

Capacity can change.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I must be fake because I can do it sometimes.
- People think I am choosing when I can and cannot cope.
- I did it yesterday, so I should manage today.
- My skills disappear under pressure.
- I am called inconsistent.
- I can do hard things in one context and simple things fail in another.
- I need help explaining why capacity changes.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain definition of uneven capacity
- examples from school, work, home, relationships, travel, appointments, and admin
- explanation of why “you did it before” is not always fair
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for explaining changing capacity
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

Uneven capacity is not the same as lying.

It means ability depends on conditions, load, recovery, body state, support, pressure, and timing.

## Suggested scripts

“I can do this sometimes, but not under every condition.”

“Yesterday does not prove today.”

“My capacity has changed.”

“I need the task adjusted to today’s capacity.”
