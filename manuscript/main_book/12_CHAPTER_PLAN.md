# Chapter 12 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 12 — Uneven Skills, Uneven Days

## Place in book

Chapter 12 follows:

- Chapter 11 — Transitions, Change, and Being Thrown Off

It continues Part 2 — What Might Be Happening.

## Chapter job

Chapter 12 should explain why ability and capacity can vary by day, context, load, support, timing, recovery, body state, sensory input, and pressure.

It should help the reader understand that being able to do something once does not always mean they can do it every time, in every place, under every condition.

## Core point

Being able to do something sometimes does not mean you can do it always.

Capacity can change.

That does not make the reader fake, lazy, or inconsistent on purpose.

## What Chapter 12 must do

- explain uneven skills and uneven capacity clearly
- avoid calling the reader fake, lazy, or unreliable
- show how context, load, support, recovery, body state, sensory input, and pressure affect ability
- explain why “you did it yesterday” may be an unfair argument
- give small ways to notice and work with changing capacity
- connect uneven days to overload, burnout, masking, demand friction, and transitions without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 2

Chapter 2 explains hidden effort and cost.

Chapter 12 explains variation: why the same person may perform differently across different days or conditions.

## Difference from Chapter 7

Chapter 7 explains burnout and longer-term depletion.

Chapter 12 explains day-to-day and context-to-context unevenness.

## Difference from Chapter 18

Chapter 18 will be about planning without collapse.

Chapter 12 is not a planning chapter.

It explains why plans must respect uneven capacity.

## Suggested structure

1. What this chapter is for
2. If you can only read one thing
3. Start here if
4. Short version
5. What uneven capacity means
6. Why “you did it before” may not prove today
7. What changes capacity
8. What makes it worse
9. What helps
10. 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes
11. Useful scripts
12. What not to force

## Avoid

- implying inconsistency is deliberate
- saying all limits are permanent
- giving too many planning systems
- turning the chapter into productivity advice
- repeating all of Chapter 2 or Chapter 7
- making the reader prove their difficulty
