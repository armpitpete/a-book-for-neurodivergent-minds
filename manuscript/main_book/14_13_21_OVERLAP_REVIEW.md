# Chapter 14, Chapter 13, and Chapter 21 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 14 against:

- Chapter 13 — Social Confusion and After-Analysis
- Chapter 21 — Communication Without Guesswork

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 14 has its own job as the rejection-sensitivity and fear-of-error chapter.

## Chapter 13 job

Chapter 13 explains social confusion and after-analysis.

Its job is:

- explain why social information can be hard to read
- explain why the mind may replay a social event afterward
- separate useful review from punishment loops
- help the reader separate facts from guesses

Chapter 13 should stay focused on unclear social information and replay.

## Chapter 14 job

Chapter 14 explains rejection sensitivity and fear of getting it wrong.

Its job is:

- explain why possible rejection, criticism, exclusion, or disapproval can feel intense
- explain why mistakes and corrections can feel unsafe
- separate feeling, fact, guess, and next step
- help the reader pause before treating fear as proof

Chapter 14 should stay focused on the alarm and the pause before action.

## Chapter 21 job

Chapter 21 will explain communication without guesswork.

Its likely job is:

- ask for clearer words
- reduce hidden meanings
- set direct communication expectations
- make repair and clarification easier

Chapter 14 can prepare for Chapter 21, but should not become the communication system chapter.

## Main distinction

Chapter 13 = what was unclear, and is this review helping or punishing me?

Chapter 14 = am I treating possible rejection or criticism as proof?

Chapter 21 = how can communication use clearer words and less guesswork?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 14 is structurally sound as the rejection-sensitivity and fear-of-error chapter.
