# Chapter 14 Brief

## Title

Rejection Sensitivity and Fear of Getting It Wrong

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain rejection sensitivity and fear of getting it wrong.

It should help the reader understand intense threat-feeling around criticism, correction, silence, delay, exclusion, disappointment, or possible mistakes.

## Core message

A feeling of rejection can be real and intense without being complete proof that rejection has happened.

Fear is information.

Fear is not always evidence.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- They hate me.
- I ruined it.
- I got it wrong again.
- I need to fix this immediately.
- Silence means rejection.
- Correction means I am bad.
- A small mistake means everything is unsafe.
- I cannot relax until I know they are not annoyed.
- I need help pausing before I react.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain definition of rejection sensitivity
- plain explanation of fear of getting it wrong
- examples of criticism, correction, silence, delay, exclusion, tone, uncertainty, and perceived mistakes
- distinction between feeling, fact, guess, and next step
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for checking, pausing, or repairing without panic
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

The feeling is real.

The conclusion may still need checking.

## Suggested scripts

“I am having a strong rejection alarm. I need to check the facts.”

“I may be guessing. Can you clarify?”

“I want to repair this if needed, but I need to understand first.”

“I need a pause before I respond.”
