# Chapter 14 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 14 — Rejection Sensitivity and Fear of Getting It Wrong

## Place in book

Chapter 14 follows:

- Chapter 13 — Social Confusion and After-Analysis

It continues Part 2 — What Might Be Happening.

## Chapter job

Chapter 14 should explain rejection sensitivity and fear of getting it wrong in plain language.

It should help the reader understand why possible criticism, rejection, exclusion, disappointment, correction, silence, or disapproval can feel intense and urgent.

It should also help the reader pause before treating fear as proof.

## Core point

A feeling of rejection can be real and intense without being complete proof that rejection has happened.

Fear is information.

Fear is not always evidence.

## What Chapter 14 must do

- explain rejection sensitivity clearly
- explain fear of getting it wrong without shame
- avoid calling the reader needy, dramatic, attention-seeking, or overreacting
- explain why criticism, correction, silence, delay, or uncertainty can feel threatening
- separate feeling, fact, guess, and next step
- give small ways to pause before reacting
- connect to social confusion, masking, after-analysis, and uneven capacity without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 13

Chapter 13 explains social confusion and after-analysis.

Chapter 14 explains the threat-feeling that can happen when the reader thinks they may have been rejected, criticised, excluded, or wrong.

The two overlap when a confusing social event becomes a rejection fear.

## Difference from Chapter 21

Chapter 21 will be about communication without guesswork.

Chapter 14 is not a communication system.

It explains the emotional alarm and gives first steps for pausing, checking, and reducing damage.

## Avoid

- making the reader feel childish or dramatic
- saying the feeling is fake
- saying the feeling is always accurate
- encouraging instant messages, apologies, or confrontation while alarmed
- turning the chapter into a full relationship guide
- repeating all of Chapter 13
