# Chapter 15, Chapter 7, Chapter 9, and Chapter 12 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 15 against:

- Chapter 7 — Burnout
- Chapter 9 — Sensory Load
- Chapter 12 — Uneven Skills, Uneven Days

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 15 has its own job as the body-basics and body-floor chapter.

## Chapter 7 job

Chapter 7 explains burnout.

Its job is:

- explain longer-term depletion
- focus on reduced demand and protected recovery
- avoid quick-fix recovery promises

Chapter 15 can mention rest and capacity, but should not become another burnout chapter.

## Chapter 9 job

Chapter 9 explains sensory load.

Its job is:

- explain sensory input as real load
- name input types
- show how input can build up
- give small ways to reduce input

Chapter 15 can mention sensory comfort as a body basic, but should not become the sensory-load chapter.

## Chapter 12 job

Chapter 12 explains uneven capacity.

Its job is:

- explain why a skill may be accessible in one condition but not another
- show why “you did it before” may not prove today
- name conditions that affect capacity
- help the reader adjust the task to current capacity

Chapter 15 can mention capacity changing, but should stay focused on body basics as one important condition.

## Chapter 15 job

Chapter 15 explains body-floor support.

Its job is:

- explain food and body basics as foundation support
- reduce shame around easy foods, safe foods, and basic care difficulty
- make body needs visible as capacity factors
- give low-demand ways to keep the floor from dropping too far

## Main distinction

Chapter 7 = what keeps draining me faster than I can recover?

Chapter 9 = what input is my system trying to process?

Chapter 12 = what changed between then and now?

Chapter 15 = has my body floor dropped?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 15 is structurally sound as the body-basics and body-floor chapter.
