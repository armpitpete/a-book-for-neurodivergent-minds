# Chapter 15 Brief

## Title

Food, Body Basics, and Keeping the Floor in Place

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain body basics as foundation support.

It should help the reader understand that food, water, sleep, pain, temperature, movement, medication, toilet needs, and sensory comfort can affect capacity.

## Core message

Body basics are not a reward for coping.

They are part of the floor that makes coping possible.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I forget to eat or drink.
- I do not notice body needs until they are urgent.
- Cooking feels too hard.
- I live on safe foods or easy foods.
- I am ashamed that basic care is difficult.
- Hunger, pain, tiredness, or temperature changes my mood and capacity.
- Other people make food and body care sound simple.
- I need a low-demand way to keep the floor from dropping.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of keeping the floor in place
- body basics: food, water, sleep, medication, pain, toilet needs, temperature, movement, and sensory comfort
- why body needs can be hard to notice
- low-demand food and care ideas
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for food/body support without shame
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

This is not diet advice.

This is capacity support.

The aim is not perfect eating.

The aim is keeping the floor from dropping too far.

## Suggested scripts

“I need food before I can think properly.”

“I am not being difficult. My body floor has dropped.”

“I need the easiest safe food, not the perfect food.”

“I need help with the first body basic: food, drink, pain, toilet, temperature, or rest.”
