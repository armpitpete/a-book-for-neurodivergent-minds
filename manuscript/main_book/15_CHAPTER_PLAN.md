## Chapter job

Chapter 15 should explain why food, water, sleep, medication, pain, toilet needs, temperature, movement, and basic body care can affect capacity.

It should help the reader see body basics as foundation support, not moral discipline.

It should reduce shame around inconsistent eating, forgetting body needs, struggling with cooking, or only managing basic food.

## Core point

Body basics are not a reward for coping.

They are part of the floor that makes coping possible.

## What Chapter 15 must do

- explain body basics in plain language
- avoid diet culture, shame, weight talk, and moralising food
- avoid making food sound like a productivity hack
- explain how hunger, thirst, pain, temperature, tiredness, medication, toilet needs, and sensory issues can affect capacity
- give small ways to keep the floor in place on hard days
- include basic food options without making them perfect or expensive
- connect body basics to overload, burnout, sensory load, demand friction, and uneven capacity without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 7

Chapter 7 explains burnout and longer-term depletion.

Chapter 15 explains daily body-floor support.

Chapter 15 can mention recovery, but should not become another burnout chapter.

## Difference from Chapter 31

Chapter 31 will be about pacing a week.

Chapter 15 is not a weekly pacing chapter.

## Avoid

- diet advice
- weight-loss framing
- moralising food choices
- saying everyone must eat the same way
- making cooking the standard of success
- shaming safe foods or easy foods
- giving too many options
- making body care sound easy when capacity is low
