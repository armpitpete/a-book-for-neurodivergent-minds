# Chapter 16, Chapter 9, Chapter 10, and Chapter 17 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 16 against:

- Chapter 9 — Sensory Load
- Chapter 10 — Demand Friction
- Chapter 17 — Routines That Support Rather Than Trap

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 16 has its own job as the environment and space-support chapter.

## Chapter 9 job

Chapter 9 explains sensory load.

Its job is:

- explain sensory input as real load
- name input types
- show how input can build up
- give small ways to reduce input

Chapter 16 can use sensory examples, but should not become the sensory-load chapter.

## Chapter 10 job

Chapter 10 explains demand friction.

Its job is:

- explain why a task can become harder when it becomes a demand
- show how requests, choices, vague steps, rushing, watching, interruption, and pressure can create friction
- separate friction from refusal
- make the first step smaller and clearer

Chapter 16 can mention setup friction, but should stay focused on space, access, and environment.

## Chapter 17 job

Chapter 17 will explain routines that support rather than trap.

Its likely job is:

- make routines flexible and supportive
- avoid all-or-nothing routine failure
- reduce repeated decision load
- keep routines small enough to use on real days

Chapter 16 can prepare for Chapter 17, but should not become a routine chapter.

## Chapter 16 job

Chapter 16 explains environment as a capacity factor.

Its job is:

- show how hostile spaces drain capacity
- make one part of the space less hostile
- reduce environmental load and setup friction
- use one-zone changes rather than whole-room projects

## Main distinction

Chapter 9 = what input is my system trying to process?

Chapter 10 = what makes this demand hard to enter?

Chapter 16 = what is this space making harder?

Chapter 17 = what routine would support me without trapping me?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 16 is structurally sound as the environment and space-support chapter.
