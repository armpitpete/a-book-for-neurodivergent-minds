# Chapter 16 Brief

## Title

Environment: Making Space Less Hostile

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain environment as a capacity factor.

It should help the reader understand that spaces can drain or support them before the task even begins.

## Core message

A hostile space can drain capacity before the task even starts.

Making one part of the space less hostile is practical support, not fussiness.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I am too fussy about space.
- I cannot start because the room is wrong.
- Clutter, noise, light, smell, or people make everything harder.
- I need things visible, but visible things also overwhelm me.
- I lose tasks because the needed item is too far away or hidden.
- I need a space that reduces friction, not a perfect room.
- I am ashamed that environment affects me this much.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of hostile space
- examples: noise, light, clutter, smell, temperature, layout, storage, distance, people, interruptions, and visual demand
- one-zone changes
- low-cost and low-demand adjustments
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for environmental adjustments
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

This is not about creating a perfect room.

It is about reducing one source of friction or load.

## Suggested scripts

“This space is using too much capacity.”

“I need to reduce one environmental load.”

“I can do the task better if the setup is easier to enter.”

“I need this item visible or reachable so the task has fewer steps.”
