# Chapter 16 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 16 — Environment: Making Space Less Hostile

## Place in book

The current working table of contents places Chapter 16 in Part 3 — What Helps in Real Life.

It follows:

- Chapter 15 — Food, Body Basics, and Keeping the Floor in Place

It comes before:

- Chapter 17 — Routines That Support Rather Than Trap

## Chapter job

Chapter 16 should explain how spaces can increase or reduce load.

It should help the reader understand that environment is not just background. Noise, light, clutter, smell, temperature, layout, people, movement, distance, storage, and visual demands can all affect capacity.

It should focus on small environment changes, not perfect rooms.

## Core point

A hostile space can drain capacity before the task even starts.

Making one part of the space less hostile is practical support, not fussiness.

## What Chapter 16 must do

- explain environment as a capacity factor
- avoid making the reader feel childish, fussy, messy, or controlling
- show how noise, light, clutter, smell, temperature, layout, distance, storage, and interruption can affect capacity
- give small ways to make a space less hostile
- include low-cost and low-demand options
- avoid making a perfect tidy room the goal
- connect environment to sensory load, demand friction, transitions, body basics, and uneven capacity without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 9

Chapter 9 explains sensory load.

Chapter 16 explains how the physical and social environment can be changed to reduce load.

Chapter 16 can use sensory examples, but should be about space design and practical adjustments.

## Difference from Chapter 17

Chapter 17 will be about routines that support rather than trap.

Chapter 16 is not a routine chapter.

It focuses on place, setup, access, and environmental friction.

- giving too many choices
