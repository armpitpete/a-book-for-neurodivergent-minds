# Chapter 17, Chapter 16, and Chapter 18 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 17 against:

- Chapter 16 — Environment: Making Space Less Hostile
- Chapter 18 — Planning Without Collapse

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 17 has its own job as the supportive-routines chapter.

## Chapter 16 job

Chapter 16 explains environment as a capacity factor.

Its job is:

- show how hostile spaces drain capacity
- make one part of the space less hostile
- reduce environmental load and setup friction
- use one-zone changes rather than whole-room projects

Chapter 17 can use environment examples, but should not become the environment chapter.

## Chapter 17 job

Chapter 17 explains routines as repeated support rails.

Its job is:

- explain routines as support rather than control
- reduce repeated decisions and setup friction
- show when routines become traps
- use anchor steps, minimum versions, and restart steps
- keep routines flexible enough for real days

Chapter 17 should not become a full planning chapter.

## Chapter 18 job

Chapter 18 will explain planning without collapse.

Its likely job is:

- make plans smaller and safer
- reduce planning overload
- avoid all-or-nothing planning
- help the reader choose practical next steps over full life rebuilds

Chapter 17 can prepare for Chapter 18, but should not take over its planning role.

## Main distinction

Chapter 16 = what is this space making harder?

Chapter 17 = what routine would support me without trapping me?

Chapter 18 = how can I plan without collapsing?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 17 is structurally sound as the supportive-routines chapter.
