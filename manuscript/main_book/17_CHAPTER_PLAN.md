# Chapter 17 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 17 — Routines That Support Rather Than Trap

## Place in book

The current working table of contents places Chapter 17 in Part 3 — What Helps in Real Life.

It follows:

- Chapter 16 — Environment: Making Space Less Hostile

It comes before:

- Chapter 18 — Planning Without Collapse

## Chapter job

Chapter 17 should explain routines as support, not control.

It should help the reader understand that routines can reduce decision load, protect body basics, support transitions, and make tasks easier to enter.

It should also explain why routines can become traps when they are too rigid, too large, too fragile, or treated as proof of worth.

## Core point

A routine should support the person.

The person should not have to suffer to protect the routine.

## What Chapter 17 must do

- explain routines in plain language
- avoid shame around failed routines
- avoid all-or-nothing routine thinking
- show how routines can reduce repeated decisions and setup friction
- show how routines can become traps when too rigid or too large
- give small ways to make a routine flexible and survivable
- connect routines to environment, demand friction, transitions, body basics, and planning without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 16

Chapter 16 explains environment and space support.

Chapter 17 explains repeated patterns of action.

The two overlap when a space is set up to support a routine.

## Difference from Chapter 18

Chapter 18 will explain planning without collapse.

Chapter 17 is not a full planning chapter.

It focuses on repeated supports that reduce friction across ordinary days.

## Avoid

- productivity culture
- perfect morning-routine advice
- moralising consistency
- saying routines always work
- making the routine more important than the person
- making every day the same
- giving too many routine templates
- taking over the planning chapter
