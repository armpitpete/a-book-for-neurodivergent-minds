# Chapter 18, Chapter 17, and Chapter 19 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 18 against:

- Chapter 17 — Routines That Support Rather Than Trap
- Chapter 19 — Decisions, Choices, and Getting Unstuck

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 18 has its own job as the planning-without-collapse chapter.

## Chapter 17 job

Chapter 17 explains routines as repeated support rails.

Its job is:

- explain routines as support rather than control
- reduce repeated decisions and setup friction
- show when routines become traps
- use anchor steps, minimum versions, and restart steps
- keep routines flexible enough for real days

Chapter 18 can mention routines, but should not become the routines chapter.

## Chapter 18 job

Chapter 18 explains planning without collapse.

Its job is:

- explain why planning can become larger than the task
- shrink the plan to current capacity
- define good enough before starting
- choose one safe next step
- set stop points so planning does not become a spiral

Chapter 18 should not become the decisions chapter.

## Chapter 19 job

Chapter 19 will explain decisions, choices, and getting unstuck.

Its likely job is:

- explain why choices can freeze the reader
- reduce choice overload
- make deciding safer and smaller
- separate real choice from fake choice or pressure
- help the reader move when stuck between options

Chapter 18 can prepare for Chapter 19, but should not take over its choice-making role.

## Main distinction

Chapter 17 = what routine would support me without trapping me?

Chapter 18 = how can I plan without collapsing?

Chapter 19 = how can I choose or get unstuck without drowning in options?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 18 is structurally sound as the planning-without-collapse chapter.
