# Chapter 18 Brief

## Title

Planning Without Collapse

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain planning without overwhelm.

It should help the reader make a plan small enough to use when capacity is limited.

It should protect the reader from turning planning into a huge task, a full life rebuild, or a reason to feel worse.

## Core message

A plan should reduce pressure, not create a new collapse.

The best plan is often the smallest next step that protects the day.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I need a plan, but planning overwhelms me.
- I make huge plans and then crash.
- I do not know where to start.
- I try to fix my whole life at once.
- My task list gets bigger than my capacity.
- I need to know what good enough means before I begin.
- I need one safe next step, not twenty options.
- I need a stop point so planning does not become another spiral.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of why planning can cause collapse
- good enough before starting
- one next step
- minimum safe plan
- stop point
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for a smaller plan or clearer next step
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

Planning is meant to make action safer and clearer.

If planning becomes bigger than the task, the plan needs shrinking.

## Suggested scripts

“I need one next step, not the whole plan.”

“What is good enough here?”

“This plan is too big for today’s capacity.”

“I need a stop point before this becomes a spiral.”
