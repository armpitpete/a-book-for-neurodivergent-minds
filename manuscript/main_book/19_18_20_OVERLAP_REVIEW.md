# Chapter 19, Chapter 18, and Chapter 20 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 19 against:

- Chapter 18 — Planning Without Collapse
- Chapter 20 — Asking for Help Without Having to Perform Distress

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 19 has its own job as the decisions, choices, and getting-unstuck chapter.

## Chapter 18 job

Chapter 18 explains planning without collapse.

Its job is:

- explain why planning can become larger than the task
- shrink the plan to current capacity
- define good enough before starting
- choose one safe next step
- set stop points so planning does not become a spiral

Chapter 19 can mention planning, but should not become the planning chapter.

## Chapter 19 job

Chapter 19 explains decisions, choices, and getting unstuck.

Its job is:

- explain why choices can freeze the reader
- reduce choice overload
- separate real choice, fake choice, pressure, and fear
- make deciding safer and smaller
- help the reader move when no option feels perfect

Chapter 19 should not become the help-asking chapter.

## Chapter 20 job

Chapter 20 will explain asking for help without having to perform distress.

Its likely job is:

- explain why asking for help can be hard
- separate clear need from visible distress
- give ways to ask before crisis
- reduce the pressure to prove suffering
- help the reader ask for practical support without performing collapse

Chapter 19 can mention asking for fewer options, but should not take over the help-asking role.

## Main distinction

Chapter 18 = how can I plan without collapsing?

Chapter 19 = how can I choose or get unstuck without drowning in options?

Chapter 20 = how can I ask for help without having to perform distress?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 19 is structurally sound as the decisions, choices, and getting-unstuck chapter.
