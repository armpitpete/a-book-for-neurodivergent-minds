# Chapter 19 Brief

## Title

Decisions, Choices, and Getting Unstuck

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain decision overload and getting unstuck.

It should help the reader understand why choices can freeze action and how to make a decision smaller, safer, and less loaded.

## Core message

A choice should help you move.

If a choice has become too big to enter, shrink the choice before blaming yourself.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I cannot choose.
- I get stuck between options.
- I freeze when there are too many choices.
- I worry about choosing wrong.
- I try to guess what everyone else wants.
- I cannot tell whether I have a real choice.
- I need a safe-enough option, not the perfect option.
- I need help moving when no option feels right.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of decision overload
- real choice / fake choice / pressure / fear distinction
- too many options
- safe-enough decision
- getting unstuck
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for fewer options or clearer choice boundaries
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

The goal is not the perfect choice.

The goal is a safe-enough choice that helps the next move happen.

## Suggested scripts

“I need fewer options.”

“Is this a real choice, or has it already been decided?”

“I need to know what matters most here.”

“I am choosing the safe-enough option so I can move.”
