# Chapter 20 Brief

## Title

Asking for Help Without Having to Perform Distress

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain asking for help before crisis.

It should help the reader ask for practical support without having to perform distress, prove suffering, or collapse visibly before being believed.

## Core message

Need should not have to become a performance before it is believed.

Asking early is practical, not attention-seeking.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- Nobody believes me unless I am visibly falling apart.
- I sound calm, so people think I am fine.
- I ask too late because I do not want to be a problem.
- I do not know how much distress I need to show.
- I need support but cannot explain it under pressure.
- I am afraid people will call me needy or dramatic.
- I need practical help, not a test of how bad I look.
- I need a way to ask before crisis.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of why asking for help can be hard
- calm does not mean fine
- need / support request / evidence / distress display distinction
- asking before crisis
- examples from home, school, work, appointments, admin, and relationships
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for practical support
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

Distress display is not the same as need.

A person may need help before they look distressed enough for others to notice.

## Suggested scripts

“I am asking early so this does not become a crisis.”

“I may sound calm, but I still need help.”

“I need practical support with the next step.”

“I do not need you to judge how distressed I look. I need help with this specific thing.”
