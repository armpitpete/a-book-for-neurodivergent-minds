# Chapter 20 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 20 — Asking for Help Without Having to Perform Distress

## Place in book

The current working table of contents places Chapter 20 in Part 3 — What Helps in Real Life.

It follows:

- Chapter 19 — Decisions, Choices, and Getting Unstuck

It comes before:

- Chapter 21 — Communication Without Guesswork

## Chapter job

Chapter 20 should explain why asking for help can be hard, especially when people only respond after visible distress.

It should help the reader ask for practical support before crisis, without having to perform collapse, prove suffering, or make distress look dramatic enough to be believed.

It should also explain why a calm or quiet request can still be serious.

## Core point

Need should not have to become a performance before it is believed.

Asking early is practical, not attention-seeking.

## What Chapter 20 must do

- explain help-asking in plain language
- avoid calling the reader needy, manipulative, weak, dramatic, or attention-seeking
- explain why people may ask too late
- explain why calm requests may still be urgent or important
- separate need, support request, evidence, and distress display
- give ways to ask for practical support before crisis
- include examples for home, school, work, appointments, admin, and relationships
- connect help-asking to rejection sensitivity, communication, decisions, planning, masking, and burnout without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 19

Chapter 19 explains choices and getting unstuck.

Chapter 20 explains asking for help when the reader cannot or should not have to solve the next step alone.

The two overlap when the reader asks someone to reduce choices or make the next step clearer.

## Difference from Chapter 21

Chapter 21 will explain communication without guesswork.

Chapter 20 can use clear scripts, but should stay focused on asking for help and being believed before visible collapse.

## Suggested structure

1. What this chapter is for

4. Short version
5. Why asking for help can be hard
6. Need, support request, evidence, and distress display
7. Calm does not mean fine
8. Asking before crisis
9. What helps
10. 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes
11. Useful scripts
12. What not to force
13. Quick summary

## Avoid

- making the reader prove pain
- implying help is only valid after collapse
- encouraging dramatic performance
- saying people should always help immediately
- taking over the communication chapter
- making every problem another person’s responsibility
- making help-asking sound easy
- too many scripts
