# Chapter 20 Standalone Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 20 — Asking for Help Without Having to Perform Distress.

## Verdict

Pass as standalone draft v0.1.

The chapter can be read on its own and still gives a useful idea.

## Core idea

Need should not have to become a performance before it is believed.

Useful reader questions:

> What help do I need before this becomes worse?

> What is the next practical support?

## What works

- Defines asking for help plainly.
- Avoids needy, weak, dramatic, and attention-seeking framing.
- Explains why asking for help can be hard.
- Separates need, support request, evidence, and distress display.
- Explains why calm does not mean fine.
- Gives early-help examples for home, school, work, appointments, admin, and relationships.
- Includes 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Includes practical scripts.
- Ends with three quick-summary bullets.

The chapter explains its core idea clearly enough on its own.
