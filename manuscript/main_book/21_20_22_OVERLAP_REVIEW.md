# Chapter 21, Chapter 20, and Chapter 22 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 21 against:

- Chapter 20 — Asking for Help Without Having to Perform Distress
- Chapter 22 — Boundaries, Exits, and Reducing Damage

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 21 has its own job as the communication-without-guesswork chapter.

## Chapter 20 job

Chapter 20 explains asking for help without having to perform distress.

Its job is:

- explain why asking for help can be hard
- separate need from visible distress
- show that calm requests can still be serious
- give ways to ask before crisis
- help the reader ask for practical support without proving collapse

Chapter 21 can include help-related scripts, but should not become the help-asking chapter.

## Chapter 21 job

Chapter 21 explains communication without guesswork.

Its job is:

- reduce hidden meanings
- ask for clear words and written steps
- make expectations explicit
- ask for specific repair information
- separate directness from rudeness
- reduce reliance on hints, tone, and guessing

Chapter 21 should not become the boundaries or exits chapter.

## Chapter 22 job

Chapter 22 will explain boundaries, exits, and reducing damage.

Its likely job is:

- explain limits and exits
- protect capacity and safety
- reduce damage during conflict, overwhelm, or unsafe situations
- help the reader leave, pause, or set limits when continuing is harmful

Chapter 21 can mention limits, but should stay focused on making meaning clear.

## Main distinction

Chapter 20 = how can I ask for help without having to perform distress?

Chapter 21 = how can communication use clearer words and less guesswork?

Chapter 22 = what boundary, pause, or exit reduces damage?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 21 is structurally sound as the communication-without-guesswork chapter.
