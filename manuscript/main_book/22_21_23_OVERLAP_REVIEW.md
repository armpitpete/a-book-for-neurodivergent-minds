# Chapter 22, Chapter 21, and Chapter 23 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 22 against:

- Chapter 21 — Communication Without Guesswork
- Chapter 23 — Repair, Apology, and Not Carrying Everything

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 22 has its own job as the boundaries, exits, and damage-reduction chapter.

## Chapter 21 job

Chapter 21 explains communication without guesswork.

Its job is:

- reduce hidden meanings
- ask for clear words and written steps
- make expectations explicit
- ask for specific repair information
- separate directness from rudeness
- reduce reliance on hints, tone, and guessing

Chapter 22 can use clear words for boundaries, but should not become the communication-clarity chapter.

## Chapter 22 job

Chapter 22 explains boundaries, exits, pauses, and reducing damage.

Its job is:

- explain limits and exits
- protect capacity, safety, and dignity
- reduce damage during conflict, overwhelm, unsafe demands, sensory overload, and communication breakdown
- help the reader pause, leave, or reduce contact when continuing would be harmful

Chapter 22 should not become the repair/apology chapter.

## Chapter 23 job

Chapter 23 will explain repair, apology, and not carrying everything.

Its likely job is:

- separate repair from self-punishment
- explain useful apology
- show what is and is not the reader's responsibility
- reduce over-apologising and carrying the whole situation alone
- support repair after damage, if repair is safe and needed

Chapter 22 can mention repair later, but should stay focused on reducing damage first.

## Main distinction

Chapter 21 = how can communication use clearer words and less guesswork?

Chapter 22 = what boundary, pause, or exit reduces damage?

Chapter 23 = what repair is needed, and what is not mine to carry?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 22 is structurally sound as the boundaries, exits, and damage-reduction chapter.
