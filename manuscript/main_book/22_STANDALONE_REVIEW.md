## Core idea

A boundary is not a failure of care.

Useful reader questions:

> Is continuing making this worse?

> What boundary, pause, or exit would reduce damage?

## What works

- Defines boundaries plainly.
- Explains exits and pauses as practical safety tools.
- Avoids framing boundaries as cruelty, rejection, selfishness, or punishment.
- Separates boundary, threat, punishment, exit, and repair.
- Explains when continuing makes damage worse.
- Includes a safety-first note.
- Gives examples for home, school, work, appointments, admin, online spaces, and relationships.
- Includes 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Includes practical scripts.
- Ends with three quick-summary bullets.
