# Chapter 23, Chapter 22, and Chapter 24 Overlap Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 23 against:

- Chapter 22 — Boundaries, Exits, and Reducing Damage
- Chapter 24 — Friendship, Love, and Being Misread

## Verdict

The overlap is acceptable.

No immediate rewrite is needed.

Chapter 23 has its own job as the repair, apology, and responsibility chapter.

## Chapter 22 job

Chapter 22 explains boundaries, exits, pauses, and reducing damage.

Its job is:

- explain limits and exits
- protect capacity, safety, and dignity
- reduce damage during conflict, overwhelm, unsafe demands, sensory overload, and communication breakdown
- help the reader pause, leave, or reduce contact when continuing would be harmful

Chapter 23 can mention repair after a pause or exit, but should not become the boundaries chapter.

## Chapter 23 job

Chapter 23 explains repair, apology, and not carrying everything.

Its job is:

- separate repair from self-punishment
- explain useful apology
- separate action, effect, intent, responsibility, and repair
- show what is and is not the reader's responsibility
- reduce over-apologising and carrying the whole situation alone
- support repair after damage, if repair is safe and needed

Chapter 23 should not become the friendship, love, and being misread chapter.

## Chapter 24 job

Chapter 24 will explain friendship, love, and being misread.

Its likely job is:

- explain relational misreading without shame
- reduce panic around friendship and love confusion
- separate care, attraction, attachment, recognition, masking, and being misunderstood
- show how relationships can be made clearer and safer

Chapter 23 can use relationship examples, but should stay focused on repair and responsibility.

## Main distinction

Chapter 22 = what boundary, pause, or exit reduces damage?

Chapter 23 = what repair is needed, and what is not mine to carry?

Chapter 24 = how can friendship, love, and being misread be understood without shame?

## Current status

No immediate rewrite needed.

Chapter 23 is structurally sound as the repair, apology, and not-carrying-everything chapter.
