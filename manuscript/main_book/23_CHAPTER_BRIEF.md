# Chapter 23 Brief

## Title

Repair, Apology, and Not Carrying Everything

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain repair and apology without self-punishment.

It should help the reader work out what needs repairing, what belongs to them, and what does not belong to them.

## Core message

Repair is about what happened and what can be done next.

It is not proof that everything is your fault.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- If someone is upset, it must be my fault.
- If I made a mistake, I am bad.
- I apologise for everything, even when I do not know what I did.
- I try to repair the whole relationship when one specific thing happened.
- I cannot tell the difference between intent and effect.
- I do not know what a useful apology sounds like.
- I carry other people's feelings as my job.
- I need to repair without collapsing into shame.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of repair
- action / effect / intent / responsibility / repair distinction
- useful apology
- what is not yours to carry
- repair after a pause, boundary, or exit
- when repair is not safe or not useful
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for specific repair and specific apology
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

A useful apology names a specific thing.

It does not require the person to apologise for existing.

## Suggested scripts

“I am sorry for that specific thing.”

“I can repair this part, but I cannot carry the whole situation alone.”

“I need to understand what needs repairing before I apologise for everything.”

“My intent was not to hurt you, but I can still look at the effect.”
