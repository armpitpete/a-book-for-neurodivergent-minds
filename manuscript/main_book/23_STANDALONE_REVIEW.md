# Chapter 23 Standalone Review

## Scope

Reviewed Chapter 23 — Repair, Apology, and Not Carrying Everything.

## Verdict

Pass as standalone draft v0.1.

The chapter can be read on its own and still gives a useful idea.

## Core idea

Repair is about what happened and what can be done next.

It is not proof that everything is your fault.

Useful reader questions:

> What specific thing needs repairing?

> What part is mine to carry?

## What works

- Defines repair plainly.
- Explains apology as one kind of repair, not the whole subject.
- Avoids making repair into self-punishment.
- Separates action, effect, intent, responsibility, and repair.
- Explains that intent does not erase effect.
- Explains useful apology as specific, not global self-blame.
- Names what is not the reader's responsibility to carry.
- Includes repair after a boundary or exit.
- Explains when repair is not safe or useful.
- Includes 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Includes practical scripts.
- Ends with three quick-summary bullets.
