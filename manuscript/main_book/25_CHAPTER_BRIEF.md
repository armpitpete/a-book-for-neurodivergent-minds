# Chapter 25 Brief

## Title

Work, School, and Being Treated Fairly

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain fairness, access needs, and practical support in work, school, college, training, volunteering, and placements.

It should help the reader separate personal failure from bad fit, unclear demands, missing support, inaccessible systems, or unfair treatment.

## Core message

Being treated fairly is not a special favour.

Support should reduce barriers so the person can do the real task.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I am failing at work or school.
- I should be able to cope like everyone else.
- I need support but do not want to be seen as difficult.
- The task may be possible, but the setup makes it harder.
- Instructions are unclear.
- Deadlines, noise, transitions, or priority changes overwhelm me.
- I do not know what adjustment would help.
- I need fairness without having to prove I am broken.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of fairness
- bad fit / unclear demand / missing support / unfair treatment distinction
- practical supports and adjustments
- asking for fairness without proving failure
- examples for instructions, deadlines, sensory load, written information, breaks, transitions, priority order, workload, and communication
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking for practical support
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

Support is not there to make the task fake or easier than everyone else's task.

Support is there to reduce barriers so the real task can be done.

## Suggested scripts

“I can do the task better if the instructions are written down.”

“I need the priority order, not all tasks treated as equally urgent.”

“The task is possible, but the setup is creating a barrier.”

“I am asking for support so I can do the work, not avoid it.”
