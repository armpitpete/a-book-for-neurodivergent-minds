# Chapter 25 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 25 — Work, School, and Being Treated Fairly

## Place in book

The current working table of contents places Chapter 25 in Part 3 — What Helps in Real Life.

It follows:

- Chapter 24 — Friendship, Love, and Being Misread

It comes before:

- Chapter 26 — Systems, Forms, and Official Language

## Chapter job

Chapter 25 should explain work, school, fairness, access needs, and practical support.

It should help the reader separate personal failure from bad fit, unclear demands, unfair treatment, inaccessible systems, sensory overload, unsupported transitions, and missing adjustments.

It should also show that needing support in work or school is not the same as being incapable.

## Core point

Being treated fairly is not a special favour.

Support should reduce barriers so the person can do the real task.

## What Chapter 25 must do

- explain fairness in plain language
- explain access needs and reasonable support without legal overcomplication
- avoid making the reader feel weak, incapable, difficult, lazy, or demanding
- separate personal failure from bad fit, unclear demand, lack of support, and unfair treatment
- show how practical adjustments can make work or school more usable
- include examples for instructions, deadlines, sensory load, written information, breaks, transitions, priority order, workload, and communication
- connect work/school fairness to masking, burnout, sensory load, planning, communication, boundaries, and systems without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 24

Chapter 24 explains personal connection and being misread in relationships.

Chapter 25 explains structured environments such as work, school, college, training, volunteering, and placements.

The two overlap when relationships affect work or school, but Chapter 25 should stay focused on fairness and access.

## Difference from Chapter 26

Chapter 26 will explain systems, forms, and official language.

Chapter 25 can mention forms and official processes, but should focus on the work/school setting and being treated fairly inside it.

## Suggested structure

1. What this chapter is for
2. If you can only read one thing
3. Start here if
4. Short version
5. What fairness means
6. Bad fit, unclear demand, missing support, and unfair treatment
7. Practical supports and adjustments
8. Asking for fairness without proving failure
9. What helps
10. 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes
11. Useful scripts
12. What not to force
13. Quick summary

## Avoid

- legal-heavy wording
- making fairness sound like a favour
- implying all workplaces or schools will respond well
- telling the reader to disclose everything without care
- making the reader responsible for fixing the whole system
- taking over the systems/forms chapter
- too many scripts
