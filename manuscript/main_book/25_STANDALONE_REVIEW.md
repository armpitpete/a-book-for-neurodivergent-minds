## Core idea

Being treated fairly is not a special favour.

Support should reduce barriers so the person can do the real task.

Useful reader questions:

> What is the actual task, and what barrier is in the way?

> What support would reduce that barrier?

## What works

- Defines fairness plainly.
- Avoids legal-heavy wording.
- Avoids making fairness sound like a favour.
- Separates real task from barrier.
- Separates bad fit, unclear demand, missing support, and unfair treatment.
- Explains support as barrier reduction, not task avoidance.
- Gives practical supports for instructions, deadlines, sensory load, written information, breaks, transitions, priority order, workload, and communication.
- Includes 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Includes practical scripts.
- Ends with three quick-summary bullets.
