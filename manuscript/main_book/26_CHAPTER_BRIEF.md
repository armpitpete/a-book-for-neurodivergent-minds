# Chapter 26 Brief

## Title

Systems, Forms, and Official Language

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain systems, forms, portals, paperwork, official wording, and bureaucracy without shame.

It should help the reader translate official language into real tasks and find the next required action.

## Core message

Official language often hides the real task.

The first job is to translate the system into one clear next action.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I am stupid because I cannot understand this form.
- I avoid portals, letters, and paperwork.
- Official wording makes me freeze.
- I cannot tell what they actually want me to do.
- I do not know what deadline matters.
- I do not know what evidence is needed.
- I do not know who to contact.
- The whole system feels too big to enter.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- plain explanation of why systems can become inaccessible
- official wording versus real task
- five things to find: deadline, required action, evidence needed, contact route, consequence
- translating forms, letters, portals, emails, policies, appointments, and official instructions
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for asking what the system actually needs
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

The system may look large, but the next useful job is usually smaller:

> What is the next required action?

## Suggested scripts

“What exactly do I need to do next?”

“What deadline matters first?”

“What evidence do you need from me?”

“Can you put the next step in plain language?”
