# Chapter 26 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 26 — Systems, Forms, and Official Language

## Place in book

The current working table of contents places Chapter 26 in Part 3 — What Helps in Real Life.

It follows:

- Chapter 25 — Work, School, and Being Treated Fairly

It comes before:

- Chapter 27 — Money, Admin, and Avoiding Shame Spirals

## Chapter job

Chapter 26 should explain systems, forms, portals, paperwork, official wording, and bureaucracy without shame.

It should help the reader translate official language into real tasks, find the next required action, and avoid being overwhelmed by the whole system at once.

It should also explain that difficulty with systems is not the same as stupidity, laziness, or refusal.

## Core point

Official language often hides the real task.

The first job is to translate the system into one clear next action.

## What Chapter 26 must do

- explain systems, forms, and official language in plain language
- avoid shaming paperwork difficulty, portal difficulty, missed forms, avoidance, or admin fear
- explain why systems can become inaccessible
- show how to translate official wording into real tasks
- show how to identify deadline, required action, evidence needed, contact route, and consequence
- include examples for forms, letters, portals, emails, policies, appointments, and official instructions
- connect systems/forms to planning, decisions, help-asking, communication, fairness, and money/admin without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 25

Chapter 25 explains fairness and support inside work, school, and structured settings.

Chapter 26 explains the systems, forms, and official language that often surround work, school, health, benefits, bills, courses, appointments, and services.

The two overlap when work or school uses official forms or procedures, but Chapter 26 should stay focused on decoding systems.

## Difference from Chapter 27

Chapter 27 will explain money, admin, and avoiding shame spirals.

Chapter 26 can mention admin, but should focus on system decoding and official wording rather than money pressure or shame spirals as the main topic.

## Avoid

- legal-heavy wording
- pretending systems are simple
- blaming the reader for avoiding confusing systems
- making the reader process the whole system at once
- giving too many form methods
- taking over the money/admin chapter
- making every official letter sound dangerous
- too many scripts
