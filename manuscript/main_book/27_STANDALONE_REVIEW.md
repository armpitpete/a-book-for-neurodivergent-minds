## Core idea

A money or admin problem needs a next action, not a character trial.

Shame usually makes the task harder to enter.

Useful reader questions:

> What is the practical problem?

> What is the next stabilising action?

## What works

- Defines money/admin tasks plainly.
- Avoids financial-advice detail and legal-heavy debt advice.
- Avoids shaming debt, missed payments, avoidance, disorganisation, unopened letters, low income, or delayed action.
- Separates practical problem, risk, next action, support, and self-blame.
- Explains shame as something that makes the task harder to enter.
- Focuses on stabilising one task rather than fixing all money/admin at once.
- Includes examples for bills, debt letters, subscriptions, banking, benefits, forms, receipts, budgeting, missed deadlines, and calls/emails.
- Includes 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Includes practical scripts.
- Ends with three quick-summary bullets.
