# Chapter 28 Brief

## Title

Health, Appointments, and Being Believed

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain health needs, appointments, describing symptoms, support needs, follow-up, and being believed.

It should help the reader prepare for appointments and communicate what is happening without needing to perform distress.

## Core message

Being calm does not mean being fine.

Health needs should be taken seriously before someone has to collapse, perform distress, or prove suffering dramatically.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I sound calm, so people think I am fine.
- I forget what I needed to say in appointments.
- I do not know how to describe symptoms or impact.
- I avoid appointments because they are stressful.
- I need written information because spoken information disappears.
- I need someone with me but feel ashamed.
- I was not believed before, so asking again feels hard.
- I need to know what happens next.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- being calm does not mean fine
- symptom / impact / risk / support need / follow-up / belief barrier distinction
- preparing a short appointment note
- asking for written information, slower explanation, advocacy, or follow-up
- examples for GP/doctor appointments, hospital appointments, mental health, medication questions, phone calls, reception/admin, and follow-up
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for appointments and being believed
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

The appointment should not depend on how distressed the person looks.

It should focus on what is happening, how it affects life, what risk exists, and what support or follow-up is needed.

## Suggested scripts

“I may sound calm, but this is affecting my life.”

“I need to use my notes so I do not forget important information.”

“Can you explain the next step in writing?”

“What should I do if this gets worse?”
