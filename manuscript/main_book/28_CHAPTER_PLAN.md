# Chapter 28 Plan

## Chosen chapter

Chapter 28 — Health, Appointments, and Being Believed

## Place in book

The current working table of contents places Chapter 28 in Part 3 — What Helps in Real Life.

It follows:

- Chapter 27 — Money, Admin, and Avoiding Shame Spirals

It comes before:

- Chapter 29 — Rest, Recovery, and Not Earning Exhaustion

## Chapter job

Chapter 28 should explain health needs, appointments, describing symptoms, support needs, follow-up, and being believed.

It should help the reader prepare for appointments, communicate what is happening, ask for reasonable support, and separate calm presentation from being fine.

It should also reduce shame around needing care, needing advocacy, asking again, taking notes, bringing someone, or asking for information in writing.

## Core point

Being calm does not mean being fine.

Health needs should be taken seriously before someone has to collapse, perform distress, or prove suffering dramatically.

## What Chapter 28 must do

- explain health appointments and being believed in plain language
- avoid giving medical diagnosis or treatment advice
- avoid shaming health anxiety, avoidance, missed appointments, needing support, or needing care
- separate symptom, impact, risk, support need, follow-up, and belief barrier
- show how to prepare a short appointment note
- show how to ask for written information, slower explanation, advocacy, or follow-up
- include examples for GP/doctor appointments, hospital appointments, mental health, medication questions, phone calls, reception/admin, and follow-up
- connect health appointments to help-asking, communication, systems/forms, shame spirals, and boundaries without retelling those chapters
- stay clear for ND and YP readers
- work as a standalone chapter

## Difference from Chapter 27

Chapter 27 explains money/admin and shame spirals.

Chapter 28 explains health needs, appointments, and being believed.

The two overlap when health appointments involve admin, bills, forms, or shame, but Chapter 28 should stay focused on health communication and support.

## Difference from Chapter 29

Chapter 29 will explain rest, recovery, and not earning exhaustion.

Chapter 28 can mention recovery, but should focus on health appointments, belief, symptoms, preparation, and follow-up.

## Avoid

- medical advice or diagnosis
- telling the reader to ignore symptoms
- telling the reader to panic about every symptom
- making appointment preparation too complex
- blaming missed appointments or avoidance on character
- taking over the rest/recovery chapter
- implying professionals always respond well
- too many scripts
