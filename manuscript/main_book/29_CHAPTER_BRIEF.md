# Chapter 29 Brief

## Title

Rest, Recovery, and Not Earning Exhaustion

## Status

Brief v0.1.

## Chapter job

This chapter should explain rest, recovery, capacity loss, and exhaustion without treating rest as laziness or failure.

It should help the reader notice early signs of capacity loss and choose a recovery action before exhaustion becomes damage.

## Core message

Rest is not something you only earn after breaking.

Recovery is part of staying able, not proof that you have failed.

## Reader need

The reader may think:

- I am lazy if I rest before collapse.
- I have to earn rest by doing enough first.
- I only stop when my body or brain forces me to stop.
- I cannot tell the difference between rest, avoidance, shutdown, and giving up.
- I need different kinds of recovery after different kinds of load.
- I feel guilty when I do less.
- I need recovery after appointments, admin, social contact, sensory load, or work/school.
- I need to rest before the damage is severe.

This chapter should explain the pattern without shame.

## Must include

- clear opening point
- short version near the top
- rest / recovery / avoidance / collapse / shutdown distinction
- different kinds of recovery: physical, sensory, emotional, social, cognitive, practical
- early signs of capacity loss
- choosing one recovery action
- examples for after appointments, work/school, social load, sensory load, admin load, emotional load, and health load
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions
- scripts for saying rest or recovery is needed
- three-bullet quick summary

## Key distinction

Rest is not the same as avoiding everything.

Useful recovery should reduce damage and help the person return to life with more capacity.

## Suggested scripts

“I need recovery before this becomes damage.”

“Rest now is not failure. It is prevention.”

“I am not refusing everything. I am reducing load so I can continue later.”

“I need a quieter recovery action, not more pressure.”
