# Chapter 31 Draft Note

## Draft completed

Chapter 31 has a first working draft.

## Chosen title

Pacing a Week Without Breaking Yourself

## Why this title was chosen

No Chapter 31 title or brief existed in the repo.

The previous chapter, Chapter 30, covered repeated breaking points and life structure.

Chapter 31 narrows the scale to one week so the reader does not have to redesign their whole life.

## Main job

Chapter 31 explains weekly pacing as capacity protection, not productivity optimisation.

## Main choices

- Framed a week as a load container, not a blank productivity grid.
- Separated fixed demands, movable demands, recovery demands, and optional extras.
- Explained stacking hard things.
- Added before-load and after-load.
- Added the minimum safe week.
- Added a simple weekly load scan.
- Kept the task small: one hard point, one moved demand, one protected basic, one margin space.
- Added 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Added practical scripts.
- Linked back to Chapters 18, 22, 29, and 30.
- Ended with three quick-summary bullets.

## Boundary notes

Chapter 30 is the wider life-pattern chapter.

Chapter 31 is the week-scale pacing chapter.

It should not become a full calendar system or productivity method.
