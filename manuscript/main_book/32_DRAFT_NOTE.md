# Chapter 32 Draft Note

## Draft completed

Chapter 32 has a first working draft.

## Chosen title

When the Plan Goes Wrong

## Why this title was chosen

No Chapter 32 title or brief existed in the repo.

Chapter 31 covered weekly pacing before the week breaks.

Chapter 32 follows naturally by helping the reader reset after a plan has already gone wrong.

## Main job

Chapter 32 explains how to respond to a failed plan without shame, panic, self-attack, or giving up completely.

## Main choices

- Framed a failed plan as information, not proof that the reader is a failure.
- Separated the plan failing, the day feeling lost, and the person not being a failure.
- Added a short reset process: stop, name, sort, choose, repair.
- Added urgency sorting: still urgent, can move, can reduce, can drop.
- Added the minimum repair step.
- Added return smaller, save the next day, do not restart perfectly, and learn one thing.
- Added 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Added practical scripts for late replies, missed tasks, moving demands, smaller versions, reset time, overload, and protecting tomorrow.
- Linked back to Chapters 22, 29, 30, and 31.
- Ended with three quick-summary bullets.

## Boundary notes

Chapter 31 teaches pacing before the week breaks.

Chapter 32 teaches what to do after the plan has already gone wrong.

It should not become a full productivity system, conflict repair chapter, or crisis plan.
