# Chapter 33 Brief — Getting Help Without Giving Up Control

## Working title

Getting Help Without Giving Up Control

## Main job

This chapter helps the reader ask for, accept, or organise help without feeling that they have failed, lost control, become a burden, or handed their whole life to someone else.

It should be practical for support from friends, family, teachers, workers, clinicians, colleagues, managers, carers, or services.

## Core idea

Help does not have to mean being taken over.

Useful help should make the next step clearer, safer, smaller, or more possible.

The reader is allowed to ask for specific help and keep choice where possible.

## Boundary with Chapter 32

Chapter 32 teaches reset after a plan has gone wrong.

Chapter 33 follows from the reset question: sometimes the next safe step is asking for help.

It should not become a crisis plan, therapy chapter, or full service-navigation chapter.

## Reader promise

By the end, the reader should be able to name one kind of help they need and ask for it in a specific, bounded way.

## Reader questions

- What kind of help do I need?
- What do I want the other person to do?
- What do I still want to decide myself?
- What would make the next step clearer, safer, smaller, or more possible?

## Tone rules

- Plain language.
- Young-person-readable.
- ND-safe.
- No shame framing.
- No “just ask for help” vagueness.
- No assumption that support is always safe or available.
- Keep the reader’s control and consent central.
- Avoid too many choices.

## Must include

- Help is not the same as failure.
- Difference between practical help, thinking help, communication help, body-doubling, emotional steadiness, advocacy, and protection.
- Good help vs unhelpful control.
- How to ask for one bounded thing.
- What to say when you do not want advice.
- What to say when you need someone to write, sit with you, explain, remind, or make the next step smaller.
- How to keep choice where possible.
- 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Scripts.

## Do not include yet

- Full safeguarding/crisis plan.
- Detailed therapy chapter.
- Detailed school/work accommodations system.
- Detailed benefits/service-navigation process.
- Conflict repair chapter.
