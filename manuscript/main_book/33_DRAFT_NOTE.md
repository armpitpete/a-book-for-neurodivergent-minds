# Chapter 33 Draft Note

## Draft completed

Chapter 33 has a first working draft.

## Chosen title

Getting Help Without Giving Up Control

## Why this title was chosen

No Chapter 33 title or brief existed in the repo.

Chapter 32 covered what to do when a plan goes wrong.

Chapter 33 follows naturally because sometimes the next safe step is asking for support.

## Main job

Chapter 33 explains how to ask for, accept, or organise help without shame, loss of control, or being taken over.

## Main choices

- Framed useful help as making the next step clearer, safer, smaller, or more possible.
- Explained that help is not failure.
- Separated different kinds of help: practical help, thinking help, communication help, body-doubling, emotional steadiness, advocacy, and protection.
- Added good help vs unhelpful control.
- Added the idea of one bounded request.
- Added ways to keep control where possible.
- Added wording for “no advice yet.”
- Added a safety caveat: not every person is safe to ask.
- Added 30 seconds / 3 minutes / 30 minutes actions.
- Added practical scripts.
- Linked back to Chapters 22, 28, 30, and 32.
- Ended with three quick-summary bullets.

## Boundary notes

Chapter 32 is the reset-after-the-plan-goes-wrong chapter.

Chapter 33 is the support-without-being-taken-over chapter.

It should not become a full crisis plan, therapy chapter, or service-navigation chapter.
