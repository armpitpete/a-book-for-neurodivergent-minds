# Truth Without Explaining

## Status

Expanded draft v0.1.

## Use this when

Use this when you need to say something true but you do not have the energy, safety, or words for a full explanation.

This tool is useful when people keep asking why, when you feel pressured to justify yourself, or when explaining more would make things worse.

## Default line

“I can give the short version: ____.”

## What this tool does

Truth Without Explaining lets you give the useful part without building a full defence.

You are allowed to be clear without giving your whole history.

Short truth is still truth.

## 30 seconds

Say one true sentence.

Examples:

- “I cannot do this today.”
- “I need written instructions.”
- “I am overloaded.”
- “I need more time.”
- “That does not work for me.”

Then stop.

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. What is true?
2. What do I need?
3. What is the next step?

Example:

- true: “I am overloaded.”
- need: “I need less talking.”
- next step: “I will reply later.”

Full sentence:

“I am overloaded. I need less talking. I will reply later.”

## 30 minutes

Write three reusable short truths:

- one for overload
- one for needing time
- one for saying no

Example:

- “I am overloaded and need to step away.”
- “I need time to process before I answer.”
- “No, I cannot take that on.”

Save them in your phone or notes.

## Useful scripts

“I can give the short version, but not the full explanation right now.”

“That is the true answer. I do not have more words for it today.”

“I am not refusing to explain. I am out of explaining capacity.”

## Quick summary

- Say the true part first.
- Do not build a whole defence when you are overloaded.
- Short truth is allowed.
