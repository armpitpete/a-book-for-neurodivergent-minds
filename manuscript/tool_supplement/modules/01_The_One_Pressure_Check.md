# Tool 1 — The One-Pressure Check

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you find one pressure that can be reduced now.

It is for moments when everything feels too much and the problem seems too large to enter.

The aim is not to solve the whole day.

The aim is to make the next few minutes less loaded.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- everything feels too much
- you cannot tell where to start
- a small thing feels impossible
- you are close to overload
- you are blaming yourself before checking the load
- the problem feels like your whole life
- you need one pressure reduced, not a complete plan

## Default line

> What is one pressure I can reduce right now?

## 30 seconds

Pick one pressure from this list:

- noise
- light
- people
- words
- choices
- time pressure
- hunger
- thirst
- pain
- heat or cold
- messages
- an unfinished task
- a decision
- a demand
- a place that feels too much

Then ask:

> Can this pressure be reduced by one step?

Examples:

- turn down one sound
- move away from one person
- close one tab
- put one task aside
- drink water
- ask for one thing in writing
- say “not now”
- sit somewhere quieter
- use fewer words

One reduction counts.

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. The pressure is: ____.
2. It is making this harder: ____.
3. One smaller version is: ____.
4. I will do this now: ____.

Example:

1. The pressure is: too many messages.
2. It is making this harder: choosing what matters.
3. One smaller version is: check only one message.
4. I will do this now: open the message from the appointment.

## 30 minutes

Make a simple pressure list.

Do not solve every item.

Just sort the load:

| Pressure | Can reduce now? | Smallest reduction |
|---|---|---|
| noise | yes/no | ____ |
| light | yes/no | ____ |
| people | yes/no | ____ |
| words | yes/no | ____ |
| choices | yes/no | ____ |
| body need | yes/no | ____ |
| time pressure | yes/no | ____ |
| task size | yes/no | ____ |

Pick only one reduction first.

After that, stop and notice whether the situation is even slightly smaller.

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “I need to reduce one pressure before I can answer.”
- “Too much is happening. I am making this smaller first.”
- “Please give me one thing at a time.”
- “I need fewer words right now.”
- “I am not refusing. I am reducing the load so I can continue.”
- “I need a quieter / clearer / smaller version.”

## Stop point

Stop after one useful reduction.

Do not turn this into a full life audit.

Do not keep searching for the perfect pressure.

The tool has worked if one thing is slightly less loaded.
