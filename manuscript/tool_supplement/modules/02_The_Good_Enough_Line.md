# Tool 2 — The Good-Enough Line

## Status

Draft v0.1.

Created from the current 12-tool supplement sequence.

This tool supports planning without collapse and safe use of the book.

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you decide what counts as enough before you start.

It is for tasks that grow too large because the finish line keeps moving.

The aim is not to do the task perfectly.

The aim is to make the task small enough to enter and clear enough to stop.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- you cannot start because the task feels too big
- you keep adding extra standards
- you do not know when you are allowed to stop
- you are trying to make a simple task perfect
- you are scared that good enough will look lazy
- you need a safe finish line before beginning
- you are using this book and starting to turn it into another demand

## Default line

> What would count as good enough for this one pass?

## 30 seconds

Finish this sentence:

> Good enough for now means: ____.

Examples:

- open the letter, not solve the whole problem
- write three bullet points, not the perfect email
- tidy one surface, not the whole room
- read one section, not the whole chapter
- send the appointment message, not explain everything
- make the page usable, not beautiful
- rest for ten minutes, not recover the whole week

Then stop when that line is met.

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. The task is: ____.
2. The risk is that I will turn it into: ____.
3. Good enough for this pass is: ____.
4. I will stop when: ____.

Example:

1. The task is: reply to the email.
2. The risk is that I will turn it into: explaining my whole situation perfectly.
3. Good enough for this pass is: answer the question and ask for the next step.
4. I will stop when: the reply is sent.

## 30 minutes

Make a simple good-enough boundary.

| Task | Good enough | Not required now | Stop point |
|---|---|---|---|
| ____ | ____ | ____ | ____ |

Example:

| Task | Good enough | Not required now | Stop point |
|---|---|---|---|
| sort paperwork | find the urgent letter | sort all papers | urgent letter found |

Do not add more columns.

Do not create a full productivity system.

This tool is a boundary, not a project.

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “Good enough for now is ____.”
- “I am doing one pass, not the final version.”
- “This only has to be usable.”
- “I will stop when ____ is done.”
- “I am not allowed to add extra standards right now.”
- “This is a support task, not a proof of worth.”

## Stop point

Stop when the good-enough line is met.

Do not move the line while you are tired.

Do not punish yourself for stopping where you said you would stop.

The tool has worked if the task has a clear enough finish line.
