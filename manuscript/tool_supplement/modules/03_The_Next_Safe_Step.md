# Tool 3 — The Next Safe Step

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you turn a large, unclear, or frightening problem into one safe next action.

It is for moments when the whole task feels too big to enter.

The aim is not to finish the whole problem.

The aim is to find one action that is small, safe, and real.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- you are stuck before starting
- you do not know what the task is asking from you
- the problem feels too large
- you are avoiding a letter, message, form, call, or appointment
- you are trying to solve everything before doing anything
- you need motion without panic
- you need one action that does not make the situation worse

## Default line

> What is the next safe step, not the whole solution?

## 30 seconds

Pick one safe step from this list:

- open the document
- read the first line
- find the deadline
- write one sentence
- send one short question
- put the item where you can see it
- take a photo of the letter
- ask someone to sit with you
- save the link
- make a note of the next action
- stop and come back at a named time

The step must be small enough to do without solving everything.

If the step still feels too large, make it smaller.

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. The problem is: ____.
2. The whole solution would be too much because: ____.
3. The next safe step is: ____.
4. After that, I will: stop / check / ask / rest / choose the next step.

Example:

1. The problem is: I have an official letter.
2. The whole solution would be too much because: I do not know what it wants yet.
3. The next safe step is: find the deadline.
4. After that, I will: write down the required action.

## 30 minutes

Use this table only if useful.

| Problem | Unsafe whole-task version | Next safe step | Stop point |
|---|---|---|---|
| ____ | ____ | ____ | ____ |

Example:

| Problem | Unsafe whole-task version | Next safe step | Stop point |
|---|---|---|---|
| benefits form | finish the whole form tonight | find National Insurance number | number found |

The next safe step should be:

- clear
- small
- possible
- not humiliating
- not dependent on perfect energy
- useful even if you stop afterward

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “I am only finding the next safe step.”
- “This does not have to solve everything.”
- “The next action is ____.”
- “I need the first required action, not the whole system.”
- “Please help me identify the next step.”
- “I can do one safe step, then stop.”

## Stop point

Stop after the next safe step is done.

Do not punish yourself for not doing the whole task.

Do not use one small success as proof that you should now do everything.

The tool has worked if the next step is clearer or safer than before.
