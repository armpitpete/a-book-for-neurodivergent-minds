# Tool 5 — The Body Floor Check

## Status

Draft v0.1.

Created from the current 12-tool supplement sequence.

This tool supports body basics, health appointments, capacity, and recovery.

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you check the body basics before turning a difficult moment into self-blame.

Body basics are not rewards for coping.

They are part of the floor that makes coping possible.

The aim is not to fix everything.

The aim is to check whether the body is missing something basic enough to make everything harder.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- everything feels harder than it should
- you are suddenly more irritable, foggy, tearful, panicky, or stuck
- you cannot tell whether the problem is emotional, sensory, physical, or practical
- you have forgotten food, drink, medication, pain relief, toilet, sleep, or temperature
- you are trying to solve a thinking problem while the body is already struggling
- you need a simple check before making a decision
- you are preparing for a health appointment and need plain notes

## Default line

> Is the body floor missing something basic?

## 30 seconds

Check these first:

- food
- drink
- toilet
- pain
- medication
- sleep or rest
- temperature
- light
- noise
- clothing comfort
- movement or stillness
- breathing space

Pick one body-floor action.

Examples:

- drink water
- eat something simple
- go to the toilet
- take prescribed medication if due
- move to a cooler or warmer place
- reduce light or noise
- sit down
- lie down
- loosen uncomfortable clothing
- write down pain or symptoms

One body-floor action counts.

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. My body may need: ____.
2. The smallest safe action is: ____.
3. I will check again after: ____ minutes.
4. If it does not improve, the next support is: ____.

Example:

1. My body may need: food and lower noise.
2. The smallest safe action is: eat toast and turn the sound down.
3. I will check again after: 15 minutes.
4. If it does not improve, the next support is: ask someone to help me decide what to do next.

## 30 minutes

Make a body-floor note.

| Body floor item | Checked? | Action |
|---|---|---|
| food | yes/no | ____ |
| drink | yes/no | ____ |
| toilet | yes/no | ____ |
| pain | yes/no | ____ |
| medication due | yes/no/not relevant | ____ |
| sleep/rest | yes/no | ____ |
| temperature | yes/no | ____ |
| sensory input | yes/no | ____ |
| movement/stillness | yes/no | ____ |

Do not make this a health diagnosis.

Use it to notice basic needs and prepare clearer next steps.

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “I need to check body basics before I decide.”
- “I may not be failing. I may be depleted.”
- “Food, drink, pain, rest, and sensory load count.”
- “I need a lower-input version before I can continue.”
- “Please help me check the basics first.”
- “I need to write down what is happening in my body.”

## Appointment note version

Use this if you need to explain a health issue:

1. What is happening: ____.
2. How long it has been happening: ____.
3. What makes it worse or better: ____.
4. How it affects daily life: ____.
5. What I need next: ____.

## Stop point

Stop after one useful body-floor action or one clear note.

Do not use the checklist to dismiss a real health need.

Do not use it to say “it was only food” or “it was only tiredness.”

Basic needs are real needs.

The tool has worked if the next few minutes are safer, clearer, or less loaded.
