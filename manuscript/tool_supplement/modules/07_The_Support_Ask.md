# Tool 7 — The Support Ask

## Status

Draft v0.1.

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you ask for one practical support.

It is for moments when you need help but cannot explain everything.

The aim is not to prove how bad things are.

The aim is to name the problem and ask for one useful support.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- you need help but do not know how to ask
- you are waiting until crisis before asking
- you are trying to explain too much
- you are afraid of sounding dramatic
- someone needs to know what would actually help
- you need an adjustment at work, school, an appointment, or home
- you need support without giving up control

## Default line

> The problem is ____. The support I need is ____.

## 30 seconds

Use this two-part ask:

1. Problem: ____.
2. Support needed: ____.

Examples:

- Problem: too many spoken instructions.
  Support needed: please write the next step down.
- Problem: I am close to overload.
  Support needed: I need a quieter place.
- Problem: I cannot start the form.
  Support needed: sit with me while I find the first section.
- Problem: I cannot speak clearly right now.
  Support needed: text only.
- Problem: I am stuck choosing.
  Support needed: give me two options.

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. What is happening: ____.
2. What makes it harder: ____.
3. What would help: ____.
4. What I do not need right now: ____.

Example:

1. What is happening: I am overloaded by the appointment information.
2. What makes it harder: it was all spoken quickly.
3. What would help: please write the next step and deadline.
4. What I do not need right now: more explanation before I have processed this.

## 30 minutes

Prepare a support ask before a meeting, appointment, or difficult conversation.

| Situation | Problem | Support needed | Words I can use |
|---|---|---|---|
| ____ | ____ | ____ | ____ |

Keep the ask practical.

A useful support ask is usually about:

- fewer words
- written instructions
- more time
- one step at a time
- a quieter place
- a clearer deadline
- help starting
- help stopping
- a named person to contact
- a written summary afterward

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “I need help with one practical step.”
- “The problem is ____. The support I need is ____.”
- “Please write that down.”
- “Please give me one instruction at a time.”
- “I need more processing time before I answer.”
- “I can do this if the next step is clear.”
- “I am not refusing. I need the access barrier reduced.”

## If speech is hard

Use a note or message:

```text
I am having trouble speaking clearly right now.
The important thing is: ____.
The support I need is: ____.
Please use short written instructions.
```

## Stop point

Stop after one clear support has been asked for.

Do not keep adding proof.

Do not turn the ask into a full defence of your life.

The tool has worked if the other person has one practical support request they can understand.
