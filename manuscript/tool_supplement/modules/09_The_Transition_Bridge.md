# Tool 9 — The Transition Bridge

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you move from one state, place, task, or demand into another without the switch becoming the whole problem.

Transitions can be hard even when both sides of the transition are ordinary.

Starting can be hard.

Stopping can be hard.

Leaving can be hard.

Coming back can be hard.

The aim is not to force a clean switch.

The aim is to build a small bridge between where you are and where you need to be next.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- you are stuck before starting
- you cannot stop a task even though you need to
- you need to leave the house
- you need to come back into a task after a break
- a plan has changed
- someone expects you to switch quickly
- you are frozen between two things
- you need a transition, not a lecture

## Default line

> What bridge do I need between this and the next thing?

## 30 seconds

Name the transition:

> I am moving from ____ to ____.

Then choose one bridge:

- warning time
- written next step
- object cue
- timer
- drink or snack
- quiet minute
- movement
- bag/checklist
- goodbye phrase
- restart note
- lower-input route

Examples:

- from bed to kitchen: sit up, drink water, stand
- from phone to shower: put phone on charger, get towel
- from work to home: write one end note, close laptop
- from house to appointment: shoes, bag, keys, leave
- from break to task: read the last sentence, write one next line

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. I am moving from: ____.
2. I am moving to: ____.
3. The hard part is: starting / stopping / leaving / returning / switching.
4. The bridge is: ____.
5. The first action is: ____.

Example:

1. I am moving from: sitting on the sofa.
2. I am moving to: leaving for the appointment.
3. The hard part is: starting and leaving.
4. The bridge is: shoes, coat, keys, then stand by the door.
5. The first action is: put shoes on.

## 30 minutes

Make a transition bridge for a repeated problem.

| Transition | Hard part | Bridge | First action | Stop point |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ____ to ____ | ____ | ____ | ____ | ____ |

Examples:

| Transition | Hard part | Bridge | First action | Stop point |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| sleep to morning | starting | water by bed | sit up | feet on floor |
| work to rest | stopping | end note | write next step | laptop closed |
| appointment to home | coming down | quiet route | headphones | home reached |

A bridge should be practical.

It should not depend on suddenly feeling ready.

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “I need warning before switching.”
- “Please give me the next step in writing.”
- “I am not refusing. I am transitioning.”
- “I need a bridge between this and the next thing.”
- “I can start if the first action is clear.”
- “I need to stop with a return point.”
- “I will come back after ____.”

## Return point

Use this when you need to pause a task:

```text
I stopped at: ____.
The next step is: ____.
I need: ____.
```

Example:

```text
I stopped at: page 2 of the form.
The next step is: find the reference number.
I need: the letter from the blue folder.
```

## Stop point

Stop after the bridge and first action are clear.

Do not redesign your whole routine.

Do not treat difficulty switching as laziness.

The tool has worked if the next move is less abrupt, less hidden, or easier to enter.
