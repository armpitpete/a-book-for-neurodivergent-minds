# Tool 10 — The Boundary/Exit Card

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you pause, leave, reduce contact, or make a situation smaller before it becomes unsafe or impossible.

A boundary is not a punishment.

An exit is not a failure.

Sometimes leaving early, pausing, or reducing contact is the safest way to keep the situation from getting worse.

The aim is not to win an argument.

The aim is to protect capacity, safety, and dignity.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- a situation is becoming too loud, fast, intense, or confusing
- someone is pushing for an answer before you can process
- you are close to overload, shutdown, or meltdown
- a conversation is becoming circular or unsafe
- you need to leave without explaining everything
- you need a pause before you can respond clearly
- you need to reduce contact without creating a bigger fight

## Default line

> I need to pause or leave before this gets worse.

## 30 seconds

Pick one boundary or exit:

- pause
- step away
- leave the room
- end the call
- stop replying for now
- move to text
- ask for written information
- reduce words
- delay the decision
- say no to one demand
- come back at a named time

Use the smallest safe exit first.

Examples:

- “I need five minutes.”
- “I cannot answer this now.”
- “Please write it down.”
- “I am leaving this room.”
- “I will reply tomorrow.”
- “This conversation is not helping. I am stopping now.”

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. The situation is: ____.
2. The risk is: overload / shutdown / argument / panic / unsafe pressure / unclear decision.
3. The boundary or exit is: ____.
4. I will return or follow up: ____.

Example:

1. The situation is: someone is asking several questions quickly.
2. The risk is: overload and saying yes when I do not understand.
3. The boundary or exit is: ask for the questions in writing.
4. I will return or follow up: after I have read them.

## 30 minutes

Prepare boundary/exit cards for repeated situations.

| Situation | Early warning sign | Boundary/exit | Return point |
|---|---|---|---|
| ____ | ____ | ____ | ____ |

Examples:

| Situation | Early warning sign | Boundary/exit | Return point |
|---|---|---|---|
| argument | repeating myself | stop talking for 20 minutes | text one clear line later |
| appointment | too much spoken information | ask for written summary | read at home |
| social visit | noise rising | leave before meltdown | message thanks later |

Keep the card short enough to use under pressure.

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “I need to pause.”
- “I cannot process this quickly.”
- “Please put that in writing.”
- “I am going to step away now.”
- “I am not able to continue this conversation safely.”
- “I will reply when I can think clearly.”
- “I need less pressure before I can answer.”
- “This is my answer for now: no / not today / not like this.”

## Boring + bounded version

Use this when someone is trying to pull you into a circular argument.

Keep the answer short, plain, and repeated if needed.

Examples:

- “I have answered that.”
- “I am not discussing this further now.”
- “The next step is in writing.”
- “No. I am stopping here.”
- “I will not continue while I am being pressured.”

Do not add more proof each time.

## Stop point

Stop after the boundary or exit has been named.

Do not keep defending the boundary until the other person agrees.

Do not use the tool to punish someone else.

Use it to make the situation safer or smaller.

The tool has worked if pressure, contact, input, or risk has reduced.
