# Tool 11 — The Misread Map

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you separate a social misread into clear parts before shame takes over.

A misread can happen around tone, face, words, silence, speed, timing, body language, or reaction.

The aim is not to prove that nobody was hurt.

The aim is not to make you responsible for everything.

The aim is to map what happened clearly enough to know whether repair, explanation, or a boundary is needed.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- someone misreads your tone, face, words, silence, speed, or reaction
- you are replaying a conversation repeatedly
- you cannot tell what you did wrong
- someone says you sounded rude, angry, blunt, cold, uncaring, or dismissive
- you meant one thing but it landed another way
- you need to repair without self-attack
- you need to separate facts, guesses, feelings, assumptions, and next steps

## Default line

> What did I mean, what did they experience, and what was assumed?

## Key words

### Intent

Intent means what you meant.

It is what you were trying to say, do, ask, or show.

Example:

> I meant: “I need time to think.”

### Impact

Impact means what happened for the other person.

It is how your words, silence, face, tone, or action landed for them.

Example:

> The impact was: “They felt ignored.”

Intent and impact can be different.

You can mean one thing, and someone else can experience another thing.

That does not automatically make you bad.

It also does not mean the other person is lying.

It means the situation needs clearer mapping.

## 30 seconds

Ask one question:

> Was I misread, did someone get hurt or confused, or both?

Do not answer with shame.

Answer with facts.

If you cannot answer yet, use this:

> I need to map this before I decide what it means.

## 3 minutes

Fill in these lines:

- I meant:
- I said or did:
- It may have looked or sounded like:
- They assumed:
- The impact was:
- The repair needed is:

Example:

- I meant: “I need time.”
- I said or did: I went quiet.
- It may have looked like: I was ignoring them.
- They assumed: I did not care.
- The impact was: They felt dismissed.
- The repair needed is: Say I needed processing time.

## 30 minutes

Use the map before a repair conversation.

Write a short repair:

1. Name what happened for them.
2. Name what you meant.
3. Say the clearer version.
4. Name the next step.

Example:

> That may have felt dismissive. I was not trying to ignore you. I needed time to process. Next time I will say that sooner.

If repair is not safe or not useful, write a boundary instead.

Example:

> I understand this was misread. I am willing to clarify once, but I cannot keep repeating the same defence.

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “I think I was misread. I need a moment to separate what I meant from how it landed.”
- “That was not what I meant, but I can see it affected you.”
- “My face or tone may not have matched what I meant.”
- “I need to map this before I explain it.”
- “I meant ____. I can see it may have sounded like ____.”
- “The clearer version is ____.”

## Stop point

Stop after the parts are mapped and one next step is chosen.

Do not keep replaying the whole conversation all night.

Do not use the map to prove you are bad.

Do not use the map to erase someone else’s experience.

The tool has worked if the situation is clearer and the next step is repair, clarification, boundary, or no further action.

## Quick summary

- Intent means what you meant.
- Impact means what happened for the other person.
- Assumptions can sit between the two.
- Map the parts before using shame as the answer.
