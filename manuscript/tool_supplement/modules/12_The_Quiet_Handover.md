# The Quiet Handover

## Status

Expanded draft v0.1.

## Use this when

Use this when you need to pass important information to someone but you have limited speech, energy, time, or processing capacity.

This can help during shutdown, overload, appointments, conflict, travel, school, work, or home stress.

## Default line

“I cannot explain fully. This is the important part: ____.”

## What this tool does

The Quiet Handover lets you give the useful information without having to perform a full conversation.

It is for moments when speech is expensive or unreliable.

You do not need to make the handover beautiful.

You need to make it clear enough.

## 30 seconds

Give one useful fact.

Examples:

- “I need quiet.”
- “I can text, not talk.”
- “I need to leave.”
- “The important thing is the appointment time.”
- “Please write the next step.”

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. What is happening?
2. What do I need?
3. What must the other person know?

Example:

“I am shutting down. I need quiet. The important thing is that I cannot answer questions right now.”

## 30 minutes

Make a handover card for future use.

Include:

- what happens when you are overloaded
- how you communicate when speech is hard
- what helps
- what makes it worse
- who to contact if needed

Keep it short enough to show on a phone.

## Useful scripts

“I cannot explain fully. This is the important part: ____.”

“I can answer by text.”

“Please write the plan down.”

“I need fewer words and one step at a time.”

## Quick summary

- Give the important part first.
- Use text, notes, pointing, or one sentence if speech is hard.
- Clear enough is better than perfect.
