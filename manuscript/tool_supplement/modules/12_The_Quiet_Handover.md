# Tool 12 — The Quiet Handover

## Status

Expanded draft v0.2.

Updated to match the current 12-tool supplement format.

This tool keeps the original “important part first” handover idea.

## What this tool is for

This tool helps you pass important information to someone when speech, energy, time, or processing capacity is limited.

It is for moments when a full explanation is not possible or would make the situation harder.

The aim is not to perform a perfect conversation.

The aim is to hand over the useful information clearly enough.

## Use this when

Use this when:

- you cannot explain fully
- speech is hard, unreliable, or expensive
- you are overloaded, shutting down, or close to meltdown
- you need someone to know the important part quickly
- an appointment, meeting, journey, or conflict needs a clear handover
- you need to use text, notes, pointing, or a phone card
- you need to protect the next day by leaving useful information behind

## Default line

> I cannot explain fully. This is the important part: ____.

## 30 seconds

Give one useful fact.

Examples:

- “I need quiet.”
- “I can text, not talk.”
- “I need to leave.”
- “The important thing is the appointment time.”
- “Please write the next step.”
- “I cannot answer questions right now.”
- “The pain is worse today.”
- “The deadline is Friday.”

One clear fact counts.

## 3 minutes

Use this shape:

1. What is happening: ____.
2. What I need: ____.
3. What the other person must know: ____.
4. What helps now: ____.

Example:

> I am shutting down. I need quiet. The important thing is that I cannot answer questions right now. Text is better than talking.

## 30 minutes

Make a handover card for future use.

Keep it short enough to show on a phone.

Include:

- what happens when you are overloaded
- how you communicate when speech is hard
- what helps
- what makes it worse
- who to contact if needed
- one sentence people should read first

Template:

```text
When I am overloaded, I may: ____.
I communicate best by: ____.
What helps: ____.
What makes it worse: ____.
The important thing to know first is: ____.
Contact / next step if needed: ____.
```

## Script or template

Use one of these:

- “I cannot explain fully. This is the important part: ____.”
- “I can answer by text.”
- “Please write the plan down.”
- “I need fewer words and one step at a time.”
- “I am not ignoring you. Speech is hard right now.”
- “The useful information is ____.”
- “Please read this before asking questions.”

## Appointment version

Use this before or during an appointment:

```text
The main problem is: ____.
It affects daily life by: ____.
I need help with: ____.
Please write down the next step.
```

## Next-day handover version

Use this when you need to protect tomorrow:

```text
I stopped at: ____.
The next useful step is: ____.
I need to remember: ____.
Do not restart from the beginning.
```

## Stop point

Stop after the important information has been handed over.

Do not keep explaining until it is perfect.

Do not use all your remaining capacity trying to make the other person fully understand everything.

Clear enough is better than complete when capacity is low.

The tool has worked if the other person has the most useful information first.

## Quick summary

- Give the important part first.
- Use text, notes, pointing, or one sentence if speech is hard.
- Clear enough is better than perfect.
- A handover is support, not a performance.
