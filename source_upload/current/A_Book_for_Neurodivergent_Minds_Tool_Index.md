# A Book for Neurodivergent Minds

## Tool Index and Script Library (v1.2.7)

Use this as the **field manual** part of the book. It’s designed for bad days: fast to scan, safe to reuse, and not dependent on diagnosis.

---

## Script rules (keep it simple)

- Pick **one default line** per situation. Repetition beats cleverness.
    
- Say it once. If needed, repeat once. Then **stop talking** or **leave**.
    
- Don’t over-explain when you’re overloaded. Short is safer.
    
- If you feel unsafe, prioritise **leaving and getting support** over “saying it right”.
    
- If speaking is hard, use the **Nonverbal/Phone Card** lines.
    

---

## Quick pick list (defaults only)

- **Pre-Signal:** “I’m listening — give me 10 seconds.”
    
- **Exit Early:** “I’m getting overloaded. I need to step out.”
    
- **Boring + Bounded:** “No thanks. I’m not doing this.”
    
- **Repair:** “That came out blunt. What I mean is: ____.”
    
- **First Stupid Step:** “First stupid step: ____.”
    
- **Switch the Door:** “One question at a time, please — 10 seconds to think.”
    

---

## Tool Index — “Start here if…”

- **People misread you (not listening / rude / angry / “off”)** → **Tool Card 01 — Pre-Signal**
    
- **Overload / shutdown / spike coming** → **Tool Card 02 — Exit Early**
    
- **Bait / provocation / nicknames / humiliation** → **Tool Card 03 — Boring + Bounded**
    
- **You need to repair after impact** → **Tool Card 04 — Repair in Four Lines**
    
- **You’re stuck and can’t start** → **Tool Card 05 — First Stupid Step**
    
- **Appointments/meetings go too fast; you leave with no plan** → **Tool Card 06 — Switch the Door**
    
- **You can’t speak** → **H) Nonverbal / Phone Card**

# Tool Cards (quick reference)

## Tool Card 01 — Pre-Signal (processing time / tone mismatch)

**Start here if:** People think you’re not listening, rude, angry, or “off”, but you’re processing.

**Default line:** “I’m listening — give me 10 seconds.”

- **30s:** Say the pre-signal line once.
    
- **3m:** Ask for **one question at a time** + a short pause before you answer.
    
- **30m:** Put your default line in your phone. Practise it once a week.
    

---

## Tool Card 02 — Exit Early (overload / shutdown)

**Start here if:** Your body is tipping into shutdown or you can feel a spike coming.

**Default line:** “I’m getting overloaded. I need to step out.”

**Optional add-on (return time)**

- “I’m getting overloaded. I need to step out. I’ll be back at __.”
    
- “I’m getting overloaded. I need to step out. I’m not continuing today.”
    
- **30s:** Name overload + leave.
    
- **3m:** Move to quiet + water + breathing. Decide if you’re returning (or not).
    
- **30m:** Build your exit plan: phrase + place + transport + one support contact.
    

---

## Tool Card 03 — Boring + Bounded (anti-bait)

**Start here if:** Someone is trying to hook you into a fight, “banter”, nicknames, or humiliation.

**Default line:** “No thanks. I’m not doing this.”

- **30s:** Flat response + boundary.
    
- **3m:** Repeat once, then end the interaction.
    
- **30m:** Choose your one boundary line and rehearse it out loud.
    

---

## Tool Card 04 — Repair in Four Lines (after impact)

**Start here if:** Your words came out sharp, you were misread, or you need to repair without self-erasing.

**Default line:** “That came out blunt. What I mean is: ____.”

- **30s:** Say line 1 + line 3. Then **stop talking**.
    
- **3m:** Say all 4 lines, in order.
    
- **30m:** Write your personal version once, so you can reuse it.
    

**The four lines (template)**

1. “That came out blunt.”
    
2. “My intent was: ____.”
    
3. “What I mean is: ____.”
    
4. “Next step: ____.” _(or “Can we do ____?”)_
    

**Repair in Four Lines (script)**  
Use this when you need to repair without over-explaining.

1. “That came out blunt.”
    
2. “My intent was: ____.”
    
3. “What I mean is: ____.”
    
4. “Next step: ____.” _(or “Can we do ____?”)_
    

**30s version (line 1 + line 3)**

- “That came out blunt.”
    
- “What I mean is: ____.”
    

---

## Tool Card 05 — First Stupid Step (activation)

**Start here if:** You want to start, but your body won’t.

**Default line:** “First stupid step: ____.”

- **30s:** Choose the tiniest physical start.
    
- **3m:** Do **5 minutes only** (timer), then stop.
    
- **30m:** Make a “starter list” of 10 tiny starts for bad days.
    

---

## Tool Card 06 — Switch the Door (appointments / meetings)

**Start here if:** Things go too fast; you forget; you get misread; you leave with no plan.

**Default line:** “One question at a time, please — 10 seconds to think.”

- **30s:** Ask for **one question at a time** + a pause.
    
- **3m:** Ask for the plan **in writing**: next step + reason.
    
- **30m:** Bring a one-page sheet: symptoms, questions, priorities, non-negotiables.

# Script Library (by situation)

## A) Processing and “spaced out”

**Default line:** “I’m listening — give me 10 seconds.”

**Alternates**

- “Give me a moment. I’ll answer properly.”
    
- “I need a short pause to think.”
    

**If challenged**

- “Yes — I’m listening. 10 seconds.”
    
- “I’m here. Pause a moment, please.”
    

---

## B) Tone and face mismatch (“you’re angry”)

**Default line:** “I’m not angry — I’m concentrating.”

**Alternates**

- “My tone can go flat when I’m thinking. It’s not anger.”
    
- “If I sound sharp, it’s stress — not you.”
    

**If challenged**

- “I’m not upset with you. I just need a moment.”
    
- “I’m calm. My face doesn’t always show it.”
    

---

## C) Overload (shutdown, sensory)

**Default line:** “I’m getting overloaded. I need to step out.”

**Alternates**

- “I need 5 minutes to reset.”
    
- “I can’t think clearly right now. I’ll come back to this.”
    

**If challenged**

- “I’m overloaded — it’s not personal.”
    
- “This is me hitting my limit. I’ll talk when I’m steadier.”
    

---

## D) Escalation (bait, conflict)

**Default line:** “I’m going to pause — I don’t want this to escalate.”

**Alternates**

- “This isn’t going well. Let’s try again later.”
    
- “I’m stepping back now. We can continue when it’s calmer.”
    

**If challenged**

- “I’m not arguing. I’m taking a break.”
    
- “I’m pausing so it doesn’t get worse.”
    

---

## E) Appointment (GP, clinician, therapist, meeting)

**Default line:** “One question at a time, please — 10 seconds to think.”

**Alternates**

- “I can lose words under stress. Please go step by step.”
    
- “I won’t remember this verbally. Can you write the plan down?”
    

**If dismissed (low-conflict)**

- “Can we write down the next step and the reason for it?”
    
- “Written steps help me follow this accurately.”
    

> Optional disclosure (use only if it helps): “I’m neurodivergent and I process more slowly under stress.”

---

## F) Work-safe clarity (meetings, tasks, conflict)

**Default line:** “Can we put the next step in writing?”

**Alternates**

- “What does ‘done’ look like?”
    
- “What’s the priority order?”
    

**If pressured**

- “Give me a moment to think. I’ll answer.”
    
- “I’ll come back in 10 minutes with a clear response.”
    

---

## G) Starting when stuck (activation)

**Default line:** “First stupid step: ____.”

**Alternates**

- “5 minutes only.”
    
- “Make it smaller. Start the smallest version.”
    

**If shame is blocking you**

- “Starting counts. Finishing can wait.”
    
- “Shame is a feeling, not a fact. Start anyway.”
    

---

## H) Nonverbal / Phone Card (when you can’t speak)

Use these when words won’t come. Put them in your phone as a note you can show.

### Option A — labelled (fast to point at)

`PROCESSING: I’m listening — give me 10 seconds. OVERLOAD: I’m getting overloaded. I need to step out. RETURN: I’ll be back at ____. APPOINTMENT: One question at a time. Written plan, please.`

### Option B — ultra-minimal (fits anywhere)

`I’m listening — 10 seconds. I’m getting overloaded. I need to step out. Back at ____. One question at a time. Write the plan.`

### Option C — if you can only show ONE line

`I’m getting overloaded. I need to step out now.`

---

## Safety note

If a line makes a situation riskier (unsafe person, unstable environment), drop the script and prioritise **leaving, getting support, and getting to safety**.
